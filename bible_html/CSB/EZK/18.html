Ezekiel 18

18
Personal Responsibility for Sin
1The word of the Lord came to me: 2“What do you mean by using this proverb concerning the land of Israel:
‘The fathers eat sour grapes,  # 18:2 Lm 5:7
and the children’s teeth are set on edge’?  # 18:2 Jr 31:29
3As I live” #— #this is the declaration of the Lord God  #— #“you will no longer use this proverb in Israel.  # 18:3 Ezk 12:23 4Look, every life belongs to me.  # 18:4 Nm 16:22; 27:16 The life of the father is like the life of the son #— #both belong to me. The person who sins is the one who will die.  # 18:4 Dt 24:16; Ezk 18:20; Rm 6:23
5“Suppose a man is righteous and does what is just and right: 6He does not eat at the mountain shrines  # 18:6 Lit the mountains, also in vv. 11,15,# 18:6 Ezk 22:9 or look to the idols of the house of Israel. He does not defile his neighbor’s wife  # 18:6 Ex 20:17–18; Lv 20:10 or approach a woman during her menstrual impurity.  # 18:6 Lv 18:19–20 7He doesn’t oppress anyone  # 18:7 Ex 22:21 but returns his collateral to the debtor.  # 18:7 Dt 24:12–13 He does not commit robbery, but gives his bread to the hungry  # 18:7 Mt 25:35–40 and covers the naked with clothing. 8He doesn’t lend at interest or for profit  # 18:8 Ex 22:25; Lv 25:35–37 but keeps his hand from injustice and carries out true justice between men.  # 18:8 Zch 8:16 9He follows my statutes and keeps my ordinances, acting faithfully. Such a person is righteous; he will certainly live.”  # 18:9 Am 5:4 This is the declaration of the Lord God.
10“But suppose the man has a violent son, who sheds blood  # 18:10 Nm 35:31 and does any of these things, 11though the father has done none of them. Indeed, when the son eats at the mountain shrines and defiles his neighbor’s wife, 12and when he oppresses the poor and needy, commits robbery, and does not return collateral, and when he looks to the idols, commits detestable acts,  # 18:12 Ezk 8:6,17 13and lends at interest or for profit, will he live? He will not live! Since he has committed all these detestable acts, he will certainly die. His death will be his own fault.  # 18:13 Lit His blood will be on him,# 18:13 Lv 20:9,11
14“Now suppose he has a son who sees all the sins his father has committed, and though he sees them, he does not do likewise. 15He does not eat at the mountain shrines or look to the idols of the house of Israel. He does not defile his neighbor’s wife. 16He doesn’t oppress anyone, hold collateral, or commit robbery. He gives his bread to the hungry and covers the naked with clothing. 17He keeps his hand from harming the poor, not taking interest or profit on a loan. He practices my ordinances and follows my statutes. Such a person will not die for his father’s iniquity. He will certainly live.
18“As for his father, he will die for his own iniquity because he practiced fraud, robbed his brother, and did among his people what was not good.  # 18:18 Ezk 3:18 19But you may ask, ‘Why doesn’t the son suffer punishment for the father’s iniquity? ’  # 18:19 Ex 20:5 Since the son has done what is just and right, carefully observing all my statutes, he will certainly live. 20The person who sins is the one who will die.  # 18:20 Ezk 18:4 A son won’t suffer punishment for the father’s iniquity, and a father won’t suffer punishment for the son’s iniquity.  # 18:20 Dt 24:16; Jn 9:2 The righteousness of the righteous person will be on him,  # 18:20 Is 3:10–11 and the wickedness of the wicked person will be on him.  # 18:20 Rm 2:6–9
21“But if the wicked person turns from all the sins he has committed,  # 18:21 Ezk 33:12,19 keeps all my statutes, and does what is just and right, he will certainly live; he will not die. 22None of the transgressions he has committed will be held against him.  # 18:22 Ezk 33:16 He will live because of the righteousness he has practiced.  # 18:22 Ps 18:20–24 23Do I take any pleasure in the death of the wicked? ”  # 18:23 Ezk 33:11 This is the declaration of the Lord God. “Instead, don’t I take pleasure when he turns from his ways and lives? 24But when a righteous person turns from his righteousness  # 18:24 Ezk 3:20; 33:18 and acts unjustly, committing the same detestable acts that the wicked do, will he live? None of the righteous acts he did will be remembered.  # 18:24 2Pt 2:20 He will die because of the treachery he has engaged in and the sin he has committed.
25“But you say, ‘The Lord’s way isn’t fair.’  # 18:25 Ezk 18:29; 33:17,20 Now listen, house of Israel: Is it my way that is unfair? Instead, isn’t it your ways that are unfair? 26When a righteous person turns from his righteousness and acts unjustly, he will die for this. He will die because of the injustice he has committed. 27But if a wicked person turns from the wickedness he has committed and does what is just and right, he will preserve his life. 28He will certainly live because he thought it over and turned from all the transgressions he had committed; he will not die. 29But the house of Israel says, ‘The Lord’s way isn’t fair.’ Is it my ways that are unfair, house of Israel? Instead, isn’t it your ways that are unfair?
30“Therefore, house of Israel, I will judge each one of you according to his ways.”  # 18:30 Ezk 7:3,8; 33:20 This is the declaration of the Lord God. “Repent and turn from all your rebellious acts, so they will not become a sinful stumbling block to you. 31Throw off all the transgressions you have committed, and get yourselves a new heart and a new spirit.  # 18:31 Jr 32:39 Why should you die, house of Israel? 32For I take no pleasure in anyone’s death.” This is the declaration of the Lord God. “So repent and live!
