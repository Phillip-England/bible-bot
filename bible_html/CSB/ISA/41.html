Isaiah 41

41
The Lord versus the Nations’ Gods
1“Be silent before me, coasts and islands!  # 41:1 Is 11:11; Hab 2:20; Zch 2:13
And let peoples renew their strength.
Let them approach; let them testify;
let’s come together for the trial.
2Who has stirred up someone from the east?  # 41:2 Is 41:25; 45:1–3; 46:11; Rv 16:12
In righteousness he calls him to serve.  # 41:2 Or Righteousness calls him to serve,# 41:2 Lit to his foot,# 41:2 Is 42:6
The Lord hands nations over to him,  # 41:2 2Ch 36:23; Ezr 1:2
and he subdues kings.
He makes them like dust with his sword,
like wind-driven stubble with his bow.
3He pursues them, going on safely,
hardly touching the path with his feet.
4Who has performed and done this,
calling the generations from the beginning?
I am the Lord, the first
and with the last  # 41:4 Is 43:10; 44:6; Rv 1:8,17; 22:13  #— #I am he.”  # 41:4 Is 43:13; 46:4; 48:12
5The coasts and islands see and are afraid,
the whole earth trembles.  # 41:5 Ps 67:7
They approach and arrive.
6Each one helps the other,
and says to another, “Take courage! ”
7The craftsman encourages the metalworker;  # 41:7 Is 40:19
the one who flattens with the hammer
encourages the one who strikes the anvil,
saying of the soldering, “It is good.”
He fastens it with nails so that it will not fall over.  # 41:7 Is 40:20; 46:7
8But you, Israel, my servant,  # 41:8 Is 42:19; 43:10; 44:1–2,21; 45:4; 48:20; 49:3; 54:17; Ezk 28:25; 37:25; Lk 1:54
Jacob, whom I have chosen,  # 41:8 Is 42:1; 43:10,20; 44:1–2; 45:4; 49:7; 65:9,15,22
descendant of Abraham, my friend  # 41:8 2Ch 20:7; Jms 2:23  —
9I brought  # 41:9 Or seized you from the ends of the earth
and called you from its farthest corners.
I said to you: You are my servant;
I have chosen you; I haven’t rejected you.  # 41:9 Is 43:5–7
10Do not fear, for I am with you;  # 41:10 Gn 26:24; 28:15; Is 43:5; Jr 1:19; 15:20; 42:11
do not be afraid, for I am your God.  # 41:10 Ezk 34:31
I will strengthen you; I will help you;
I will hold on to you with my righteous right hand.  # 41:10 Ex 15:6,12; Ps 18:35; 48:10; 63:8; 138:7; 139:10
11Be sure that all who are enraged against you
will be ashamed and disgraced;  # 41:11 Is 45:24
those who contend with you
will become as nothing and will perish.
12You will look for those who contend with you,
but you will not find them.
Those who war against you
will become absolutely nothing.
13For I am the Lord your God,  # 41:13 Is 43:3
who holds your right hand,
who says to you, “Do not fear,
I will help you.
14Do not fear, you worm Jacob,  # 41:14 Jb 25:6; Ps 22:6
you men  # 41:14 LXX reads small number; DSS read dead ones of Israel.
I will help you” —
this is the  Lord’s declaration.
Your Redeemer  # 41:14 Is 43:14; 44:6,24; 47:4; 48:17; 49:7,26; 54:5,8; 59:20; 60:16; 63:16 is the Holy One of Israel.  # 41:14 Is 1:4
15See, I will make you into a sharp threshing board,
new, with many teeth.
You will thresh mountains and pulverize them
and make hills into chaff.  # 41:15 Mc 4:13; Hab 3:12
16You will winnow them  # 41:16 Jr 51:2
and a wind will carry them away,
a whirlwind will scatter them.
But you will rejoice in the  Lord;
you will boast in the Holy One of Israel.
17The poor and the needy seek water, but there is none;
their tongues are parched with thirst.
I will answer them.  # 41:17 Is 43:20; 44:3; 49:10; 55:1
I am the Lord, the God of Israel. I will not abandon them.
18I will open rivers on the barren heights,  # 41:18 Is 30:25
and springs in the middle of the plains.
I will turn the desert into a pool
and dry land into springs.  # 41:18 Ps 107:35; Is 35:6–7; 43:19
19I will plant cedar, acacia, myrtle, and olive trees
in the wilderness.  # 41:19 Is 35:1; 55:13; 60:13
I will put juniper, elm, and cypress trees together
in the desert,
20so that all may see and know,
consider and understand,
that the hand of the  Lord has done this,  # 41:20 Jb 12:9; Is 66:14
the Holy One of Israel has created  # 41:20 Is 4:5  it.
21“Submit your case,” says the  Lord.
“Present your arguments,” says Jacob’s King.
22“Let them come and tell us
what will happen.  # 41:22 Is 44:7–8; 45:21; 46:10
Tell us the past events,
so that we may reflect on them
and know the outcome,
or tell us the future.
23Tell us the coming events,  # 41:23 Is 42:9; 45:3; Jn 13:19
then we will know that you are gods.
Indeed, do something good or bad,
then we will be in awe  # 41:23 DSS read we may hear when we see it.
24Look, you are nothing  # 41:24 Ps 115:8; Is 44:9; 1Co 8:4
and your work is worthless.
Anyone who chooses you is detestable.
25“I have stirred up one from the north,  # 41:25 Jr 50:3 and he has come,
one from the east  # 41:25 Is 41:2; 46:11 who invokes my  # 41:25 DSS read his  name.  # 41:25 Ezr 1:2–4
He will march over rulers as if they were mud,
like a potter who treads the clay.
26Who told about this from the beginning,
so that we might know,
and from times past,
so that we might say, ‘He is right’?
No one announced it,
no one told it,
no one heard your words.
27I was the first to say to Zion,  # 41:27 Lit First to Zion
‘Look! Here they are! ’
And I gave Jerusalem a herald with good news.  # 41:27 Is 40:9; 44:28; 52:7; Nah 1:15
28When I look, there is no one;
there is no counselor among them;
when I ask them, they have nothing to say.  # 41:28 Is 46:7
29Look, all of them are a delusion;  # 41:29 DSS, Syr read are nothing
their works are nonexistent;
their images are wind and emptiness.
