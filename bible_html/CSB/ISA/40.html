Isaiah 40

40
God’s People Comforted
1“Comfort, comfort my people,”  # 40:1 Is 12:1; 49:13; 51:3,12; 52:9; 61:2; 66:13; Jr 31:10–14; Zph 3:14–17; 2Co 1:4
says your God.
2“Speak tenderly to  # 40:2 Lit Speak to the heart of  Jerusalem,
and announce to her
that her time of hard service is over,
her iniquity  # 40:2 Is 13:11 has been pardoned,  # 40:2 Lv 26:41,43; Is 27:9
and she has received from the  Lord’s hand
double for all her sins.”  # 40:2 Ex 22:7–8; Jr 16:18; Zch 9:12; Rv 18:6
3A voice of one crying out:
Prepare the way of the  Lord in the wilderness;  # 40:3 Mal 3:1; 4:5–6
make a straight highway for our God in the desert.  # 40:3 Mt 3:3; Mk 1:3; Lk 3:4; Jn 1:23
4Every valley will be lifted up,
and every mountain and hill will be leveled;
the uneven ground will become smooth
and the rough places, a plain.  # 40:4–5 Lk 3:5–6
5And the glory of the  Lord will appear,  # 40:5 Is 35:2
and all humanity  # 40:5 Lit flesh together will see it,  # 40:5 Is 52:10; Jl 2:28
for the mouth of the  Lord has spoken.  # 40:5 Is 1:20; 34:16; 58:14
6A voice was saying, “Cry out! ”
Another said,  # 40:6 DSS, LXX, Vg read I said “What should I cry out? ”
“All humanity is grass,  # 40:6–8 1Pt 1:24–25
and all its goodness is like the flower of the field.  # 40:6 Jb 14:2; Ps 102:11; 103:15
7The grass withers, the flowers fade
when the breath  # 40:7 Or wind, or Spirit of the  Lord blows on them;  # 40:7 Lit it
indeed, the people are grass.
8The grass withers, the flowers fade,  # 40:8 Jms 1:11
but the word of our God remains forever.”  # 40:8 Is 55:11; 59:21; Mk 13:31; Lk 21:33
9Zion, herald of good news,
go up on a high mountain.  # 40:9 Is 52:7
Jerusalem, herald of good news,  # 40:9 Is 61:1
raise your voice loudly.
Raise it, do not be afraid!
Say to the cities of Judah,
“Here is your God! ”  # 40:9 Is 25:9; 35:2
10See, the Lord God comes with strength,
and his power establishes his rule.  # 40:10 Is 59:16–18
His wages are with him,  # 40:10 Is 62:11; Rv 22:12
and his reward accompanies him.
11He protects his flock like a shepherd;  # 40:11 Jr 31:10; Ezk 34:12–14,23,31; Mc 5:4; Jn 10:11,14–16
he gathers the lambs in his arms
and carries them in the fold of his garment.
He gently leads those that are nursing.
12Who has measured the waters in the hollow of his hand
or marked off the heavens with the span of his hand?  # 40:12 Is 48:13; Heb 1:10–12
Who has gathered the dust of the earth in a measure
or weighed the mountains on a balance
and the hills on the scales?
13Who has directed  # 40:13 Or measured, or comprehended the Spirit of the  Lord,
or who gave him counsel?  # 40:13 Rm 11:34; 1Co 2:16
14Who did he consult?
Who gave him understanding  # 40:14 Jb 21:22; Col 2:3
and taught him the paths of justice?
Who taught him knowledge
and showed him the way of understanding?
15Look, the nations are like a drop in a bucket;
they are considered as a speck of dust on the scales;
he lifts up the islands like fine dust.
16Lebanon’s cedars are not enough for fuel,
or its animals enough for a burnt offering.  # 40:16 Ps 50:9–11; Mc 6:6–7; Heb 10:5–9
17All the nations are as nothing before him;
they are considered by him
as empty nothingness.  # 40:17 Is 29:7
18With whom will you compare God?
What likeness will you set up for comparison with him?  # 40:18 Ex 8:10; 15:11; 1Sm 2:2; Is 40:25; 46:5; Mc 7:18; Ac 17:29
19An idol? #— #something that a smelter casts
and a metalworker plates with gold
and makes silver chains for?  # 40:19 Is 2:20; 30:22; 41:7; 44:10
20A poor person contributes wood for a pedestal
that will not rot.  # 40:20 Or who is too poor for such an offering, or who chooses mulberry wood as a votive gift; Hb obscure
He looks for a skilled craftsman
to set up an idol that will not fall over.  # 40:20 1Sm 5:3; Is 44:9–15; 46:6–7; Jr 10:3–5
21Do you not know?  # 40:21 Ps 19:1; 50:6; Is 37:26; Ac 14:17; Rm 1:19–20
Have you not heard?
Has it not been declared to you
from the beginning?
Have you not considered
the foundations of the earth?  # 40:21 Is 48:13; 51:13
22God is enthroned above the circle of the earth;
its inhabitants are like grasshoppers.  # 40:22 Nm 13:33
He stretches out the heavens like thin cloth  # 40:22 Jb 9:8; Is 37:16; 42:5; 44:24
and spreads them out like a tent to live in.  # 40:22 Jb 36:29; Ps 18:11; 19:4; 104:2
23He reduces princes to nothing  # 40:23 Jb 12:21; Ps 107:40; Is 34:12
and makes judges of the earth like a wasteland.
24They are barely planted, barely sown,
their stem hardly takes root in the ground
when he blows on them and they wither,
and a whirlwind carries them away like stubble.  # 40:24 Ps 1:4; Is 41:2,16; 64:6; Ezk 17:10,24; Nah 1:4
25“To whom will you compare me,  # 40:25 Is 46:5; Ac 17:29
or who is my equal? ” asks the Holy One.
26Look up and see!
Who created  # 40:26 Is 42:5; 48:12–13 these?
He brings out the stars by number;
he calls all of them by name.  # 40:26 Ps 147:4–5
Because of his great power and strength,
not one of them is missing.
27Jacob, why do you say,
and Israel, why do you assert,
“My way is hidden from the  Lord,
and my claim is ignored by my God”?  # 40:27 Is 7:13; 49:4,14
28Do you not know?
Have you not heard?
The Lord is the everlasting God,  # 40:28 Gn 21:33; Ps 90:2
the Creator of the whole earth.
He never becomes faint or weary;
there is no limit to his understanding.  # 40:28 Ps 147:5; Rm 11:33
29He gives strength to the faint
and strengthens the powerless.
30Youths may become faint and weary,
and young men stumble and fall,
31but those who trust in the  Lord
will renew their strength;  # 40:31 Jb 17:9; Ps 103:5; 2Co 4:8–10,16; 12:9
they will soar on wings like eagles;  # 40:31 Ex 19:4; Dt 32:11; Lk 18:1; 2Co 4:1,16; Gl 6:9; Heb 12:3
they will run and not become weary,
they will walk and not faint.
