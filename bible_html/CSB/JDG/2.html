Judges 2

2
Pattern of Sin and Judgment
1The angel of the Lord   # 2:1 Gn 22:11; Ex 3:2; Ps 34:7; 35:5–6 went up from Gilgal to Bochim and said, “I brought you out of Egypt and led you into the land  # 2:1 Ex 6:8; Nm 14:3,8; Ezk 20:15 I had promised to your ancestors.  # 2:1 Nm 14:23; Dt 6:10,23; 10:11; Jos 1:6 I also said: I will never break my covenant with you. 2You are not to make a covenant  # 2:2 Ex 23:32; Dt 7:2 with the inhabitants of this land. You are to tear down their altars.  # 2:2 Ex 34:13; Dt 7:5; 12:3; Jdg 6:30–32; 2Ch 34:7 But you have not obeyed me. What have you done? 3Therefore, I now say: I will not drive out these people before you.  # 2:3 Ex 23:29 They will be thorns  # 2:3 Lit traps in your sides,  # 2:3 Nm 33:55 and their gods will be a trap for you.”  # 2:3 Ex 23:33; 34:12; Dt 7:16; Jos 23:13 4When the angel of the Lord had spoken these words to all the Israelites, the people wept loudly. 5So they named that place Bochim  # 2:5 Or Weeping and offered sacrifices there to the Lord.
Joshua’s Death
6Previously, when Joshua had sent the people away, the Israelites had gone to take possession of the land, each to his own inheritance.  # 2:6 Dt 9:4; 11:31; Jos 1:11; 18:3; Jdg 18:9; Neh 9:15 7The people worshiped the Lord throughout Joshua’s lifetime and during the lifetimes of the elders who outlived Joshua. They had seen all the Lord’s great works  # 2:7 Dt 11:7 he had done for Israel.
8Joshua son of Nun, the servant of the Lord, died at the age of 110. 9They buried him in the territory of his inheritance, in Timnath-heres, in the hill country of Ephraim,  # 2:9 Jos 19:50; 1Sm 1:1; 14:22; 1Ch 6:67 north of Mount Gaash. 10That whole generation was also gathered to their ancestors. After them another generation rose up who did not know the Lord   # 2:10 Ex 5:2; 1Sm 2:12 or the works he had done for Israel.
11The Israelites did what was evil in the Lord’s sight.  # 2:11 Nm 32:12; Dt 4:25; 31:29; 1Kg 11:6 They worshiped the Baals  # 2:11 Jdg 3:7; 8:33; 1Sm 12:10; 2Kg 17:16; Jr 2:23; 9:14 12and abandoned the Lord, the God of their ancestors, who had brought them out of Egypt. They followed other gods from the surrounding peoples  # 2:12 Dt 6:14; 13:7 and bowed down to them. They angered the Lord,  # 2:12 1Kg 14:15; 15:30; 2Kg 17:11; 2Ch 28:25 13for they abandoned him and worshiped Baal and the Ashtoreths.  # 2:13 Jdg 10:6; 1Sm 7:3–4; 12:10; 1Kg 11:5,33; 2Kg 23:13
14The Lord’s anger burned against Israel, and he handed them over to marauders who raided them. He sold  # 2:14 Jdg 3:8; 4:2,9; 10:7; Ps 44:12; Is 50:1; Ezk 30:12; Jl 3:8 them to the enemies around them, and they could no longer resist their enemies. 15Whenever the Israelites went out, the Lord was against them  # 2:15 Dt 2:15 and brought disaster on them, just as he had promised and sworn to them.  # 2:15 Lv 26:14–46; Dt 28:15–68 So they suffered greatly.
16The Lord raised up  # 2:16 Jdg 3:9,15; 1Kg 11:14; 14:14 judges, who saved them from the power of their marauders, 17but they did not listen to their judges. Instead, they prostituted  # 2:17 Dt 4:30; 1Sm 13:6; 30:6; 2Sm 22:7; Jb 20:22; Ps 59:16; Hs 5:15 themselves with other gods, bowing down to them. They quickly turned from the way  # 2:17 Ex 32:8; Dt 9:12,16 of their ancestors, who had walked in obedience to the Lord’s commands. They did not do as their ancestors did. 18Whenever the Lord raised up a judge for the Israelites, the Lord was with  # 2:18 Gn 39:2,21; Jos 6:27; 1Sm 18:12 him and saved the people from the power of their enemies while the judge was still alive. The Lord was moved to pity whenever they groaned because of those who were oppressing and afflicting them. 19Whenever the judge died, the Israelites would act even more corruptly  # 2:19 Gn 6:12; Dt 31:29; Is 1:4 than their ancestors, following other gods to serve them and bow in worship to them. They did not turn from their evil practices or their obstinate  # 2:19 Ex 32:9; Dt 9:6,13; Ezk 2:4 ways.
20The Lord’s anger burned against Israel, and he declared, “Because this nation has violated my covenant  # 2:20 Jos 7:11,15; 23:16; Jr 34:18; Hs 6:7 that I made with their ancestors  # 2:20 Jdg 3:4; 1Kg 8:58; 2Kg 17:13; Ps 78:5; Jr 11:4 and disobeyed me, 21I will no longer drive out before them any of the nations Joshua left  # 2:21 Jos 13:1–7; 23:1–16 when he died.  # 2:21 Jos 24:29–30 22I did this to test Israel and to see whether or not they would keep the Lord’s way  # 2:22 Gn 18:19; 2Sm 22:22; Ps 138:5 by walking in it, as their ancestors had.”  # 2:22 Jos 24:31 23The Lord left these nations and did not drive them out immediately. He did not hand them over to Joshua.
