Micah 2

2
Oppressors Judged
1Woe to those who dream up wickedness
and prepare evil plans  # 2:1 Ps 36:4; Is 32:7; Nah 1:11 on their beds!
At morning light  # 2:1 Hs 7:6–7 they accomplish it
because the power is in their hands.  # 2:1 Gn 31:29; Dt 28:32; Pr 3:27
2They covet fields  # 2:2 Jr 22:17; Am 8:4 and seize them;  # 2:2 Is 5:8
they also take houses.
They deprive a man of his home,  # 2:2 1Kg 21:1–15
a person of his inheritance.  # 2:2 Ex 20:17; Is 5:8
3Therefore, the Lord says:
I am now planning  # 2:3 Dt 28:48; Jr 18:11 a disaster
against this nation;  # 2:3 Jr 8:3; Am 3:1–2
you cannot free your necks from it.  # 2:3 Lm 1:14; 5:5
Then you will not walk so proudly  # 2:3 Is 2:11–12
because it will be an evil time.  # 2:3 Am 5:13
4In that day one will take up  # 2:4 Hab 2:6 a taunt against you
and lament mournfully,  # 2:4 Jr 9:10,17–21; Mc 1:8  saying,
“We are totally ruined!  # 2:4 Is 6:11; 24:3; Jr 4:13
He measures out the allotted land of my people.
How he removes it from me!
He allots our fields  # 2:4 Jr 6:12; 8:10 to traitors.”
5Therefore, there will be no one
in the assembly of the  Lord
to divide the land by casting lots.  # 2:5 Lit Lord stretching the measuring line by lot,# 2:5 Nm 34:13,16–29; Jos 18:4,10
God’s Word Rejected
6“Quit your preaching,”  # 2:6 Is 30:10; Am 2:12; 7:16 they  # 2:6 = the prophets  preach.
“They should not preach these things;  # 2:6 Is 29:10; Mc 3:6
shame will not overtake us.”  # 2:6 Text emended; MT reads things. Shame will not depart,# 2:6 Mc 6:16
7House of Jacob, should it be asked,
“Is the Spirit of the  Lord  impatient?  # 2:7 Is 50:2; 59:1
Are these the things he does? ”
Don’t my words bring good  # 2:7 Ps 119:65,68,116; Jr 15:16
to the one who walks uprightly?  # 2:7 Ps 15:2; 84:11
8But recently my people have risen up
like an enemy:  # 2:8 Jr 12:8
You strip off the splendid robe  # 2:8 Mc 3:2–3; 7:2–3
from those who are passing through confidently,  # 2:8 Ps 120:6–7
like those returning from war.
9You force  # 2:9 Jr 10:20 the women of my people
out of their comfortable homes,
and you take my blessing  # 2:9 Perhaps the land,# 2:9 Ezk 39:21; Hab 2:14
from their children forever.
10Get up and leave,
for this is not your place of rest  # 2:10 Dt 3:19–20; 12:8–10
because defilement  # 2:10 Ps 106:38 brings destruction —
a grievous destruction!  # 2:10 Lv 18:25–28
11If a man comes
and utters empty lies  # 2:11 Jr 5:13,31  #—
“I will preach to you about wine and beer”  # 2:11 Is 28:7  —
he would be just the preacher for this people!  # 2:11 Is 30:10–11
The Remnant Regathered
12I will indeed gather  # 2:12 Mc 4:6–7 all of you, Jacob;
I will collect the remnant of Israel.  # 2:12 2Kg 19:31; Mc 5:7–8; 7:18
I will bring them together like sheep in a pen,
like a flock in the middle of its pasture.
It will be noisy with people.  # 2:12 Jr 23:3; Ezk 36:35–38
13One who breaks open the way
will advance before them;
they will break out, pass through the city gate,
and leave by it.
Their King will pass through before them,
the Lord as their leader.
