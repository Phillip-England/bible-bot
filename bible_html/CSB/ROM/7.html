Romans 7

7
An Illustration from Marriage
1Since I am speaking to those who know the law, brothers and sisters,  # 7:1 Rm 1:13 don’t you know that the law rules over someone as long as he lives? 2For example, a married woman is legally bound to her husband while he lives.  # 7:2 1Co 7:39 But if her husband dies, she is released from the law regarding the husband. 3So then, if she is married to another man while her husband is living, she will be called an adulteress. But if her husband dies, she is free from that law. Then, if she is married to another man, she is not an adulteress.
4Therefore, my brothers and sisters, you also were put to death  # 7:4 Rm 6:2 in relation to the law  # 7:4 Rm 8:2; Gl 2:19; 5:18 through the body of Christ  # 7:4 Col 1:22 so that you may belong to another. You belong to him who was raised from the dead in order that we may bear fruit for God. 5For when we were in the flesh,  # 7:5 Rm 8:8–10; 2Co 10:3 the sinful passions aroused through the law were working in us  # 7:5 Lit in our members,# 7:5 Rm 6:13,21,23 to bear fruit for death. 6But now we have been released from the law, since we have died to what held us, so that we may serve in the newness of the Spirit  # 7:6 Rm 2:29 and not in the old letter of the law.
Sin’s Use of the Law
7What should we say then?  # 7:7 Rm 3:5 Is the law sin? Absolutely not!  # 7:7 Lk 20:16 But I would not have known sin if it were not for the law.  # 7:7 Rm 4:15; 5:20 For example, I would not have known what it is to covet if the law had not said, Do not covet.   # 7:7 Ex 20:17,# 7:7 Ex 20:17 8And sin, seizing an opportunity through the commandment,  # 7:8 Rm 3:20 produced in me coveting of every kind. For apart from the law sin is dead.  # 7:8 1Co 15:56 9Once I was alive apart from the law, but when the commandment came, sin sprang to life again 10and I died. The commandment that was meant for life  # 7:10 Lv 18:5; Lk 10:28; Rm 10:5; Gl 3:12 resulted in death for me. 11For sin, seizing an opportunity through the commandment, deceived me,  # 7:11 Gn 3:13 and through it killed me. 12So then, the law is holy,  # 7:12 1Tm 1:8 and the commandment is holy and just and good. 13Therefore, did what is good become death to me? Absolutely not!  # 7:13 Lk 20:16 But sin, in order to be recognized as sin, was producing death in me through what is good, so that through the commandment, sin might become sinful beyond measure.
The Problem of Sin in Us
14For we know that the law is spiritual,  # 7:14 1Co 3:1 but I am of the flesh,  # 7:14 Or unspiritual sold  # 7:14 1Kg 21:20,25; 2Kg 17:17; Rm 6:6; Gl 4:3 as a slave under sin.  # 7:14 Rm 3:9 15For I do not understand what I am doing,  # 7:15 Jn 15:15 because I do not practice what I want to do,  # 7:15 Gl 5:17 but I do what I hate. 16Now if I do what I do not want to do, I agree with the law that it is good. 17So now I am no longer the one doing it, but it is sin living in me. 18For I know that nothing good lives in me, that is, in my flesh.  # 7:18 Jn 3:6; Rm 8:3 For the desire to do what is good is with me, but there is no ability to do it. 19For I do not do the good that I want to do, but I practice the evil that I do not want to do. 20Now if I do what I do not want, I am no longer the one that does it, but it is the sin that lives in me. 21So I discover this law:  # 7:21 Or principle,# 7:21 Rm 8:2 When I want to do what is good,  # 7:21 Or I find with respect to the law that when I want to do good evil is present with me. 22For in my inner self  # 7:22 Lit inner man I delight in God’s law,  # 7:22 2Co 4:16; Eph 3:16; 1Pt 3:4 23but I see a different law in the parts of my body,  # 7:23 Lit my members,# 7:23 Rm 6:19; Gl 5:17; Jms 4:1; 1Pt 2:11 waging war against the law of my mind and taking me prisoner to the law of sin in the parts of my body. 24What a wretched man I am! Who will rescue me from this body of death?  # 7:24 Rm 6:6; 8:2; Col 2:11 25Thanks be to God through Jesus Christ our Lord!  # 7:25 1Co 15:57 So then, with my mind I myself am serving the law of God, but with my flesh, the law of sin.
