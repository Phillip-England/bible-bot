Hannah’s Vow
1There was a man from Ramathaim-zophim  # 1:1 1Sm 1:19 in  # 1:1 Or from Ramathaim, a Zuphite from the hill country of Ephraim.  # 1:1 Jos 17:14–18; Jdg 17:1 His name was Elkanah  # 1:1 1Ch 6:22–28,33–38 son of Jeroham, son of Elihu, son of Tohu, son of Zuph, an Ephraimite. 2He had two wives,  # 1:2 Dt 21:15–17 the first named Hannah  # 1:2 Lk 2:36 and the second Peninnah. Peninnah had children, but Hannah was childless. 3This man would go up from his town every year  # 1:3 Ex 34:23; Dt 16:16; Lk 2:41 to worship and to sacrifice  # 1:3 Ex 23:14–17 to the Lord of Armies at Shiloh,  # 1:3 Jos 18:1 where Eli’s two sons, Hophni and Phinehas, were the Lord’s priests.
4Whenever Elkanah offered a sacrifice, he always gave portions of the meat  # 1:4 Dt 12:17–18 to his wife Peninnah and to each of her sons and daughters. 5But he gave a double  # 1:5 Or gave only one; Hb obscure portion  # 1:5 Gn 43:34; 48:22 to Hannah, for he loved her even though the Lord had kept her from conceiving. 6Her rival would taunt her severely just to provoke her, because the Lord had kept Hannah from conceiving. 7Year after year, when she went up to the Lord’s house,  # 1:7 Jos 18:1 her rival taunted her in this way. Hannah would weep and would not eat. 8“Hannah, why are you crying? ” her husband, Elkanah, would ask. “Why won’t you eat? Why are you troubled? Am I not better to you than ten sons? ”  # 1:8 Ru 4:15
9On one occasion, Hannah got up after they ate and drank at Shiloh.  # 1:9 LXX adds and presented herself before the Lord The priest Eli was sitting on a chair by the doorpost of the Lord’s temple.  # 1:9 1Sm 3:3 10Deeply hurt, Hannah prayed to the Lord and wept with many tears.  # 1:10 1Sm 30:6; 2Kg 4:27 11Making a vow,  # 1:11 Nm 30:6–11 she pleaded, “Lord of Armies, if you will take notice of your servant’s affliction,  # 1:11 Gn 29:32 remember and not forget me, and give your servant a son, I will give him to the Lord all the days of his life, and his hair will never be cut.”  # 1:11 Lit and no razor will go up on his head,# 1:11 Nm 6:5; Jdg 13:5; 16:7
12While she continued praying in the Lord’s presence, Eli watched her mouth. 13Hannah was praying silently,  # 1:13 Gn 24:42–45 and though her lips were moving, her voice could not be heard. Eli thought she was drunk 14and said to her, “How long are you going to be drunk?  # 1:14 Ac 2:4,13 Get rid of your wine! ”
15“No, my lord,” Hannah replied. “I am a woman with a broken heart. I haven’t had any wine or beer; I’ve been pouring out my heart before the Lord.  # 1:15 Ps 62:8 16Don’t think of me as a wicked woman;  # 1:16 1Sm 2:12 I’ve been praying from the depth of my anguish and resentment.”  # 1:16 Lk 6:45
17Eli responded, “Go in peace,  # 1:17 1Sm 25:35; 2Kg 5:19; Mk 5:34 and may the God of Israel grant the request you’ve made of him.”  # 1:17 Ru 2:13
18“May your servant find favor with you,”  # 1:18 Ru 2:13 she replied. Then Hannah went on her way; she ate and no longer looked despondent.  # 1:18 Lit and her face was not to her again,# 1:18 Rm 15:13
Samuel’s Birth and Dedication
19The next morning Elkanah and Hannah got up early to worship before the Lord. Afterward, they returned home to Ramah.  # 1:19 1Sm 1:1; 2:11 Then Elkanah was intimate with his wife Hannah, and the Lord remembered her.  # 1:19 Gn 8:1; 30:22; 1Sm 1:11 20After some time,  # 1:20 Lit In the turning of the days Hannah conceived and gave birth to a son. She named him Samuel,  # 1:20 In Hb, the name Samuel sounds like the phrase “requested from God.” because she said, “I requested him from the Lord.”
21When Elkanah and all his household went up to make the annual sacrifice  # 1:21 Dt 12:11; 1Sm 1:3 and his vow offering to the Lord, 22Hannah did not go and explained to her husband, “After the child is weaned, I’ll take him to appear in the Lord’s presence  # 1:22 Lk 2:22 and to stay there permanently.”  # 1:22 1Sm 1:11,28
23Her husband, Elkanah, replied, “Do what you think is best,  # 1:23 Nm 30:10–11 and stay here until you’ve weaned him. May the Lord confirm your  # 1:23 DSS, LXX, Syr; MT reads his word.”  # 1:23 Nm 30:13 So Hannah stayed there and nursed her son until she weaned him. 24When she had weaned him, she took him with her to Shiloh, as well as a three-year-old bull,  # 1:24 DSS, LXX, Syr; MT reads Shiloh with three bulls half a bushel  # 1:24 Lit bull and an ephah of flour, and a clay jar of wine.  # 1:24 Nm 15:9–10 Though the boy was still young,  # 1:24 Lit And the youth was a youth she took him to the Lord’s house at Shiloh.  # 1:24 Jos 18:1; 1Sm 4:3–4 25Then they slaughtered the bull and brought the boy to Eli.
26“Please, my lord,” she said, “as surely as you live,  # 1:26 2Kg 2:2,4,6 my lord, I am the woman who stood here beside you praying to the Lord. 27I prayed for this boy,  # 1:27 1Sm 1:11–13 and since the Lord gave me what I asked him for,  # 1:27 1Sm 1:17,20 28I now give the boy to the Lord. For as long as he lives, he is given to the Lord.”  # 1:28 1Sm 1:11 Then he  # 1:28 DSS read she; some Hb mss, Syr, Vg read they worshiped the Lord there.  # 1:28 LXX reads Then she left him there before the Lord
