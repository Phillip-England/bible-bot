Wives and Husbands
1In the same way, wives,  # 3:1 Eph 5:22–33; Col 3:18–19; 1Tm 3:11 submit yourselves to your own husbands so that, even if some disobey the word,  # 3:1 1Co 7:16; 1Pt 1:24–25 they may be won over  # 3:1 1Co 9:19–22; Php 3:8 without a word by the way their wives live  # 3:1 2Pt 3:11 2when they observe your pure, reverent lives.  # 3:2 Ti 2:5 3Don’t let your beauty consist of outward things like elaborate hairstyles and wearing gold jewelry or fine clothes,  # 3:3 Is 3:18–24; 1Tm 2:9 4but rather what is inside the heart  # 3:4 Or rather, the hidden person of the heart,# 3:4 Rm 2:29; 7:22  #— #the imperishable quality of a gentle  # 3:4 Mt 5:5 and quiet  # 3:4 1Tm 2:2 spirit, which is of great worth in God’s sight. 5For in the past, the holy  # 3:5 Ps 20:6; 1Co 7:14 women who put their hope  # 3:5 1Tm 5:5; 1Pt 1:3 in God also adorned themselves in this way, submitting to their own husbands, 6just as Sarah  # 3:6 Gn 17:15–21; 18:6–15; Heb 11:11 obeyed Abraham, calling him lord. You have become her children when you do what is good and do not fear any intimidation.  # 3:6 Pr 3:25
7Husbands,  # 3:7 Mt 1:19; 1Co 14:35; Eph 5:22–28; Col 3:18–19; Ti 2:4–5 in the same way, live with your wives in an understanding way, as with a weaker partner,  # 3:7 1Th 4:4 showing them honor as coheirs of the grace of life, so that your prayers will not be hindered.
Do No Evil
8Finally, all of you be like-minded and sympathetic, love one another,  # 3:8 Heb 13:1 and be compassionate  # 3:8 Eph 4:32 and humble,  # 3:8 Other mss read courteous 9not paying back evil for evil  # 3:9 Rm 12:17 or insult for insult but, on the contrary, giving a blessing,  # 3:9 Lk 6:28; Rm 12:14; 1Co 4:12 since you were called for this, so that you may inherit a blessing.

and his lips from speaking deceit,   # 3:10 Rm 3:13; Jms 3:5–8

and his ears are open to their prayer.
But the face of the Lord is against
those who do what is evil.   # 3:10–12 Ps 34:12–16
Undeserved Suffering
13Who then will harm you if you are devoted to what is good? 14But even if you should suffer for righteousness,  # 3:14 Mt 6:33; Rm 1:17; 2Pt 1:1 you are blessed. Do not fear them   # 3:14 Or Do not fear what they fear,# 3:14 Jn 6:20; 7:13or be intimidated,   # 3:14 Is 8:12,# 3:14–15 Is 8:12–13 15but in your hearts regard  # 3:15 Or sanctify, or set apart Christ  # 3:15 Other mss read set God the Lord as holy, ready at any time to give a defense to anyone who asks you for a reason for the hope that is in you.  # 3:15 1Jn 4:4 16Yet do this with gentleness and reverence, keeping a clear conscience,  # 3:16 Ac 23:1; 24:16; Rm 9:1; 2Co 1:12; 1Tm 1:5,19; 3:9; Heb 10:22; 1Pt 3:21 so that when you are accused,  # 3:16 Other mss read when they speak against you as evildoers those who disparage your good conduct in Christ will be put to shame. 17For it is better to suffer  # 3:17 Php 1:29; 1Pt 2:19 for doing good, if that should be God’s will, than for doing evil.
