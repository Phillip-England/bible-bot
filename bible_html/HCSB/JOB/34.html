Job 34

34
1Then Elihu continued, # Lit answered saying:
2Hear my words, you wise men,
and listen to me, you knowledgeable ones.
3Doesn’t the ear test words
as the palate tastes food?
4Let us judge for ourselves what is right;
let us decide together what is good.
5For Job has declared, “I am righteous,
yet God has deprived me of justice. # Jb 10:7; 27:2; 33:9
6Would I lie about my case? # Jb 6:28; 24:25; 27:4
My wound # Lit arrow is incurable,
though I am without transgression.”
7What man is like Job?
He drinks derision # Jb 15:16; Pr 19:28 like water.
8He keeps company with evildoers
and walks with wicked men.
9For he has said, “A man gains nothing
when he becomes God’s friend.” # Ex 5:2; Jb 21:15; Mal 3:14
10Therefore listen to me, you men of understanding.
It is impossible for God to do wrong,
and for the Almighty to act unjustly. # Dt 32:4; Ps 92:15; Rm 9:14
11For He repays # Dt 7:10; Jb 21:31; Is 59:18 a person according to his deeds,
and He brings his ways on him.
12Indeed, it is true that God does not act wickedly
and the Almighty does not pervert justice. # 2Ch 19:6; Jb 8:3; Ps 9:16
13Who gave Him authority over the earth?
Who put Him in charge of the entire world? # Jb 38:4; Ps 50:12; Is 40:12-14
14If He put His mind to it
and withdrew the spirit # Ps 104:29; Ec 3:19; 12:7 and breath He gave,
15every living thing would perish together
and mankind would return to the dust. # Gn 2:7; Jb 4:19; 10:9
16If you have understanding, hear this;
listen to what I have to say.
17Could one who hates justice govern the world?
Will you condemn the mighty Righteous One, # Gn 18:25; 2Sm 23:3; Rm 3:5
18who says to a king, “Worthless man! ”
and to nobles, “Wicked men! ”? # Ex 22:28; Jb 12:21; Ps 107:40
19God is not partial # Dt 10:17; Jb 13:8; 32:21 to princes
and does not favor the rich over the poor,
for they are all the work of His hands.
20They die suddenly in the middle of the night;
people shudder, then pass away.
Even the mighty are removed without effort.
21For His eyes watch over a man’s ways,
and He observes all his steps. # Jb 14:16; 31:4; Pr 5:21
22There is no darkness, no deep darkness,
where evildoers can hide themselves. # Ps 139:12; Am 9:2-3; Heb 4:13
23God does not need to examine a person further,
that one should # Some emend to God has not appointed a time for man to approach Him in court.
24He shatters the mighty without an investigation
and sets others in their place. # Jb 12:19; Ps 2:9; Dn 2:21
25Therefore, He recognizes their deeds # Jb 11:11; Ps 10:14
and overthrows them by night, and they are crushed.
26In full view of the public, # Lit In a place of spectators
He strikes them for their wickedness,
27because they turned aside from following Him
and did not understand any of His ways
28but caused the poor to cry out to Him,
and He heard the outcry of the afflicted. # Ex 3:7; 22:23; Jms 5:4
29But when God is silent, who can declare Him guilty?
When He hides His face, who can see Him?
Yet He watches over both individuals and nations,
30so that godless men should not rule
or ensnare the people.
31Suppose someone says to God,
“I have endured my punishment;
I will no longer act wickedly.
32Teach me what I cannot see;
if I have done wrong, I won’t do it again.”
33Should God repay you on your terms
when you have rejected His?
You must choose, not I!
So declare what you know.
34Reasonable men will say to me,
along with the wise men who hear me,
35“Job speaks without knowledge; # Jb 35:16; 38:2; 42:3
his words are without insight.”
36If only Job were tested to the limit,
because his answers are like those of wicked men.
37For he adds rebellion to his sin;
he scornfully claps # Nm 24:10; Jb 27:23; Lm 2:15 in our presence,
while multiplying # Jb 16:4; 35:16; Ezk 35:13 his words against God.
