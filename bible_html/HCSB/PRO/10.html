Proverbs 10

10
A Collection of Solomon’s Proverbs
1Solomon’s proverbs: # Pr 1:1
A wise son brings joy to his father,
but a foolish son, heartache to his mother. # Pr 15:20; 17:25; 23:24-25; 29:3,15
2Ill-gotten gains do not profit anyone,
but righteousness rescues from death. # Pr 11:4,18; 12:28; 21:6; Ezk 7:19; Lk 12:19-20
3The Lord will not let the righteous go hungry, # Ps 37:25; Mt 6:33
but He denies the wicked what they crave. # Ps 112:10
4Idle hands make one poor,
but diligent hands bring riches. # Pr 12:24,27; 13:4,11; 19:15; 21:5
5The son who gathers during summer is prudent;
the son who sleeps during harvest is disgraceful. # Pr 6:6-11; 30:25
6Blessings are on the head of the righteous,
but the mouth of the wicked conceals violence. # Pr 10:11
7The remembrance of the righteous is a blessing,
but the name of the wicked will rot. # Ps 9:5-6; 112:6
8A wise heart accepts commands, # Mt 7:24
but foolish lips will be destroyed.
9The one who lives with integrity lives securely, # Ps 23:4; Pr 2:7; 3:23; 19:1; 20:7; 28:6,18; Is 33:15-16
but whoever perverts his ways will be found out. # Pr 26:26; Mt 10:26; 1Tm 5:25
10A sly wink of the eye # Ps 35:19; Pr 6:13; 16:30 causes grief,
and foolish lips will be destroyed.
11The mouth of the righteous is a fountain of life, # Ps 37:30; Pr 13:14; 14:27; 16:22
but the mouth of the wicked conceals violence. # Pr 10:6
12Hatred stirs up conflicts,
but love covers all offenses. # Pr 17:9; Jms 5:20; 1Pt 4:8
13Wisdom is found on the lips of the discerning,
but a rod is for the back of the one who lacks sense. # Pr 19:29; 26:3
14The wise store up knowledge,
but the mouth of the fool hastens destruction. # Pr 10:8; 12:13; 13:3; 18:7
15A rich man’s wealth is his fortified city; # Ps 52:7; Pr 18:11
the poverty of the poor is their destruction.
16The labor of the righteous leads to life;
the activity of the wicked leads to sin. # Pr 11:19
17The one who follows instruction is on the path to life,
but the one who rejects correction goes astray. # Pr 5:12-13,22-23; 6:23; 13:13-14
18The one who conceals hatred has lying lips,
and whoever spreads slander is a fool. # Pr 11:13
19When there are many words, sin is unavoidable,
but the one who controls his lips is wise. # Pr 17:27-28; Jms 1:19; 3:2
20The tongue of the righteous is pure silver;
the heart of the wicked is of little value. # Pr 8:19; 10:32
21The lips of the righteous feed many,
but fools die for lack of sense. # Hs 4:6
22The Lord’s blessing enriches, # Gn 24:35; 26:12; Dt 8:18; Jms 1:17
and struggle adds nothing to it. # Or and He adds no trouble to it # Mt 6:31-32
23As shameful conduct is pleasure for a fool, # Pr 2:14; 15:21
so wisdom is for a man of understanding.
24What the wicked dreads will come to him, # Pr 1:26-27; Is 66:4; Heb 10:27
but what the righteous desire will be given to them. # Ps 37:4; 145:19; Pr 11:23; Mt 5:6; 1Jn 5:14-15
25When the whirlwind passes,
the wicked are no more, # Jb 21:18; Is 29:5-7
but the righteous are secure forever. # Ps 15:5; Pr 10:30; 12:3,7; Mt 7:24-27
26Like vinegar to the teeth and smoke to the eyes, # Pr 26:6
so the slacker is to the one who sends him on an errand.
27The fear of the Lord prolongs life, # Lit Lord adds to days # Pr 3:2
but the years of the wicked are cut short. # Jb 15:32-33; Pr 14:26-27; 15:16; 19:23
28The hope of the righteous is joy, # Pr 11:23; 23:18; Jr 29:11
but the expectation of the wicked comes to nothing. # Jb 8:13; 14:9; Pr 11:7; Ezk 37:11
29The way of the Lord is a stronghold for the honorable,
but destruction awaits the malicious. # Pr 13:6; 21:15
30The righteous will never be shaken,
but the wicked will not remain on the earth. # Ps 21:7; 37:29; 125:1; Pr 2:21-22; 12:3
31The mouth of the righteous produces wisdom, # Ps 37:30
but a perverse tongue will be cut out. # Ps 37:38; 120:2-4
32The lips of the righteous know what is appropriate, # Ec 10:12
but the mouth of the wicked, only what is perverse. # Pr 2:12; 10:20; 15:28
