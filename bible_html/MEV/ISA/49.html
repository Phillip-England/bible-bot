Isaiah 49

49
The Servant of the Lord
1Listen to me, O coastlands,
and pay attention, you peoples from afar.
The Lord called me from the womb; # Isa 42:1–4; Jer 1:5
from the body of my mother He named me.
2He has made my mouth like a sharp sword; # Isa 11:4; Rev 1:16
in the shadow of His hand He has hidden me
and made me a select arrow;
in His quiver He has hidden me.
3He said to me, “You are My servant, # Zec 3:8
Israel, in whom I will be glorified.” # Isa 44:23
4Then I said, “I have labored in vain; # Isa 65:23
I have spent my strength for nothing and vanity,
yet surely the justice due to me is with the Lord,
and my reward with my God.”
5Now says the Lord,
who formed me from the womb to be His servant,
to bring Jacob back to Him,
so that Israel might be gathered to Him
(yet I am honored in the eyes of the Lord, # Mt 17:5
and my God is my strength),
6He says,
“It is a light thing that you should be My servant
to raise up the tribes of Jacob
and to restore the preserved ones of Israel;
I will also make you a light to the nations # Isa 42:6; Lk 2:32
so that My salvation may reach to the ends of the earth.”
7Thus says the Lord,
the Redeemer of Israel, and his Holy One,
to the despised one, to the one whom the nation abhors, # Isa 53:3
to the servant of rulers:
“Kings shall see and arise, # Isa 52:15
princes also shall worship,
because of the Lord who is faithful
and the Holy One of Israel who has chosen you.”
The Restoration of Israel
8Thus says the Lord:
In an acceptable time I have heard you, # Ps 69:13; 2Co 6:2
and in a day of salvation I have helped you;
and I will preserve you, and give you
as a covenant of the people, # Isa 42:6
to restore the earth,
to make them inherit the desolate lands,
9saying to the prisoners, “Go forth,” # Isa 42:7; 61:1
to those who are in darkness, “Show yourselves.”
They shall feed along the paths,
and their pastures shall be in all desolate heights;
10they shall neither hunger nor thirst,
neither shall the heat nor sun strike them;
for He who has mercy on them shall lead them,
even by the springs of water He shall guide them. # Rev 7:16–17
11I will make all My mountains a road,
and My highways shall be raised up. # Isa 11:16; 62:10
12Behold, these shall come from afar;
and behold, these from the north and from the west, # Isa 43:5–6
and these from the land of Syene.
13Sing, O heavens!
And be joyful, O earth!
And break forth into singing, O mountains! # Isa 44:23
For the Lord has comforted His people
and will have mercy on His afflicted. # Isa 51:3
14But Zion said, “The Lord has forsaken me,
and the Lord has forgotten me.” # Isa 40:27
15Can a woman forget her nursing child,
and have no compassion on the son of her womb?
Even these may forget,
yet I will not forget. # Isa 44:21
16Behold, I have inscribed you on the palms of My hands; # SS 8:6
your walls are continually before Me; # Hag 2:23
17your builders hurry;
your destroyers and those who devastated you shall depart from you.
18Lift up your eyes and look all around;
all these gather themselves together and come to you.
As I live, says the Lord,
you shall surely put on all of them as ornaments
and bind them on you as a bride does.
19For your waste and your desolate places,
and the land of your destruction,
shall even now be too narrow by reason of the inhabitants,
and those who swallowed you up shall be far away.
20The children whom you shall have,
after you have lost the others,
shall say again in your ears,
“The place is too cramped for me;
make room for me that I may dwell here.”
21Then you shall say in your heart,
“Who bore these for me,
since I have lost my children and am barren,
a captive and a wanderer?
And who has brought these up?
Behold, I was left alone;
from where have these come?”
22Thus says the Lord God:
Behold, I will lift up My hand to the nations
and set up My standard to the peoples;
and they shall bring your sons in their arms,
and your daughters shall be carried on their shoulders;
23kings shall be your foster fathers
and their queens your nursing mothers; # Isa 60:3, 16
they shall bow down to you with their faces toward the ground
and lick up the dust of your feet.
And you shall know that I am the Lord,
for those who wait for Me shall not be ashamed.
24Can the prey be taken from the mighty
or the captives of a tyrant be delivered? # Mt 12:29
25For thus says the Lord:
Even the captives of the mighty shall be taken away,
and the prey of the tyrant shall be delivered;
for I will contend with him who contends with you,
and I will save your sons. # Jer 50:33–34
26I will feed those who oppress you with their own flesh, # Isa 9:20
and they shall be drunk with their own blood as with sweet wine.
And all flesh shall know # Eze 39:7
that I, the Lord, am your Savior
and your Redeemer, the Mighty One of Jacob.
