Mark 14

14
The Plot to Kill Jesus
Mt 26:1–5; Lk 22:1–2; Jn 11:45–53
1Now the feasts of the Passover and of Unleavened Bread were two days away. # Lk 22:1–2; Jn 13:1 And the chief priests and the scribes looked for a way to seize Him secretly and kill Him. 2But they said “Not on the feast day, lest there will be an uproar among the people.”
The Anointing at Bethany
Mt 26:6–13; Jn 12:1–8
3While He was in Bethany # Mt 21:17 in the house of Simon the leper, as He sat at supper, a woman came with an alabaster jar of ointment, a very costly spikenard. She broke the jar and poured the ointment on His head. # Lk 7:37–39
4There were some with indignation within themselves, saying, “Why was this ointment wasted? 5It might have been sold for more than three hundred denarii # 14:5 A year’s wages. and given to the poor.” # Jn 13:29 And they grumbled against her.
6Jesus said, “Leave her alone. Why do you trouble her? She has done a good work for Me. 7You always have the poor with you, # Dt 15:11; Mt 26:11 and whenever you wish, you may do good to them. But you will not always have Me. 8She has done what she could. # 2Co 8:12 She has come beforehand to anoint My body for burial. 9Truly I say to you, wherever this gospel will be preached throughout the whole world, # Mk 16:15 what she has done will also be spoken of as a memorial to her.”
Judas Agrees to Betray Jesus
Mt 26:14–18; Lk 22:3–6
10Then Judas Iscariot, who was one of the twelve, went to the chief priests to betray Him to them. # Mt 26:14–16; Jn 13:2 11When they heard it, they were glad and promised to give him silver. So he looked for how he might conveniently betray Him.
The Passover with the Disciples
Mt 26:17–28; Lk 22:7–14, 21–23; Jn 13:21–30
12On the first day of Unleavened Bread, when they sacrificed the Passover lamb, # Ex 12:6, 8 His disciples said to Him, “Where do You want us to prepare for You to eat the Passover?”
13So He sent two of His disciples and said to them, “Go into the city, and a man carrying a pitcher of water will meet you there. Follow him. 14Wherever he enters, say to the owner of the house, ‘The Teacher # Jn 11:28 says, Where is the guest room where I may eat the Passover with My disciples?’ 15He will show you a large upper room, # Ac 1:13 furnished and ready. Make preparations for us there.”
16His disciples went out, and came into the city, and found it as He had told them. And they prepared the Passover.
17In the evening He came with the twelve. # Lk 22:14 18As they sat and ate, Jesus said, “Truly I say to you, one of you who is eating with Me will betray Me.” # Ps 41:9; Jn 6:70
19They began to be sorrowful and to say to Him one by one, “Is it I?” and another, “Is it I?”
20He said to them, “It is one of the twelve, one who is dipping bread in the dish with Me. # Jn 13:26 21The Son of Man indeed goes as it is written concerning Him, # Mk 14:49 but woe to that man by whom the Son of Man is betrayed! # Lk 17:1 It would have been good for that man if he had never been born.”
The Lord’s Supper
Mt 26:26–30; Lk 22:15–20; 1Co 11:23–25
22As they were eating, Jesus took bread, blessed and broke it, and gave it to them, # Mt 14:19 saying, “Take and eat it. This is My body.”
23Then He took the cup, # 1Co 10:16 and when He had given thanks, He gave it to them. And they all drank from it.
24He said to them, “This is My blood of the new covenant, # Ex 24:8; Zec 9:11 which is shed for many. 25Truly I say to you, I will drink no more of the fruit of the vine until that day when I drink it new in the kingdom of God.” # Mt 26:29
26When they had sung a hymn, they went out to the Mount of Olives. # Mt 21:1; 26:30
Peter’s Denial Foretold
Mt 26:31–35; Lk 22:31–34; Jn 13:36–38
27Jesus said to them, “All of you will fall away on account of Me this night, for it is written:
‘I will strike the shepherd,
and the sheep will be scattered.’ # Zec 13:7; Mt 26:31 # 14:27 Zec 13:7.
28But after I have risen, I will go before you to Galilee.” # Mt 28:16; Mk 16:7
29Peter said to Him, “Even if all fall away, I will not.”
30Jesus said to him, “Truly, I say to you that this day, during the night, before the cock crows twice, you will deny Me three times.” # Mk 14:66–72; Jn 13:38
31But he said more vehemently, “If I must die with You, I will not deny You.” They all said the same thing.
The Prayer in Gethsemane
Mt 26:36–46; Lk 22:39–46
32They came to a place which was named Gethsemane. And He said to His disciples, “Sit here while I pray.” # Mk 14:36 33He took Peter and James and John with Him # Mk 5:37; 9:2 and began to be greatly distressed and very troubled. 34And He said to them, “My soul is deeply sorrowful unto death. # Jn 12:27 Remain here and keep watch.”
35He went a little farther and fell on the ground and prayed that, if it were possible, the hour might pass from Him. # Mk 14:41; Heb 5:7 36He said, “Abba, Father, # Gal 4:6 all things are possible for You. Remove this cup from Me; yet not what I will, but what You will.” # Jn 5:30
37Then He came and found them sleeping and said to Peter, “Simon, are you sleeping? Could you not keep watch one hour? 38Watch and pray, lest you enter into temptation. The spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak.” # Mt 26:41
39Again He went away and prayed the same words. 40When He returned, He again found them sleeping, for their eyes were heavy. And they did not know what to answer Him.
41When He returned a third time, He said to them, “Are you still sleeping and taking your rest? It is enough! The hour has come. # Mk 14:35 Look, the Son of Man is betrayed into the hands of sinners. # Mk 9:31 42Rise up, let us go. Look! He who betrays Me is at hand.”
The Betrayal and Arrest of Jesus
Mt 26:47–56; Lk 22:47–53; Jn 18:3–12
43Immediately, while He was still speaking, Judas, # Ac 1:16 one of the twelve, and with him a great crowd with swords and clubs, came from the chief priests and the scribes and the elders.
44Now he who betrayed Him had given them a sign, saying, “Whomever I kiss, He is the One. Seize Him and lead Him away safely.” 45So as soon as he came, he went to Him immediately and said, “Rabbi, Rabbi!” and kissed Him. 46They laid hands on Him and seized Him. 47Then one of those who stood by drew his sword and struck the servant of the high priest and cut off his ear.
48Then Jesus said to them, “Have you come out with swords and clubs to arrest Me as you would a thief? 49Every day I was with you in the temple teaching, # Mk 12:35; Jn 8:2 and you did not seize Me. But the Scriptures must be fulfilled.” 50Then they all deserted Him and fled. # Ps 38:11; Mk 14:27
The Young Man Who Fled
51A young man followed Him, wearing a linen cloth around himself. And the young men laid hold of him, 52so he left the linen cloth, and fled from them unclothed.
Jesus Before the Sanhedrin
Mt 26:57–68; Lk 22:54–55, 63–71; Jn 18:13–14, 19–24
53They led Jesus away to the high priest. And all the chief priests and the elders and the scribes were assembled with him. 54Peter followed Him at a distance into the courtyard of the high priest. # Mt 26:3 He sat with the guards and warmed himself by the fire. # Jn 18:18
55Now the chief priests and the entire Sanhedrin requested testimony against Jesus to put Him to death, but found none. 56Many bore false witness against Him, but their testimonies did not agree.
57Then some rose up and bore false witness against Him, # Mt 26:60–61 saying, 58“We heard Him say, ‘I will destroy this temple that is made with hands, and within three days I will build another not made with hands.’ ” # Mk 15:29; Jn 2:19 59But still their testimony did not agree.
60Then the high priest stood up in the midst and asked Jesus, “Do You answer nothing? What is it which these men testify against You?” 61But He kept silent and answered nothing. # Isa 53:7
Again the high priest asked Him, “Are You the Christ, the Son of the Blessed One?” # Mt 16:16
62Jesus said, “I am. And you will see the Son of Man sitting at the right hand of Power and coming with the clouds of heaven.” # Ps 110:1; Heb 1:3
63The high priest tore his robes, # Nu 14:6 saying, “What need do we have of any further witnesses? 64You have heard the blasphemy. What do you think?”
They all condemned Him as guilty unto death. # Lev 24:16 65Then some began to spit on Him, and to blindfold Him, and to strike Him, # Isa 50:6 saying to Him, “Prophesy!” And the guards struck Him with the palms of their hands. # Mt 26:67–68
Peter’s Denial of Jesus
Mt 26:69–75; Lk 22:56–62; Jn 18:15–18, 25–27
66While Peter was below in the courtyard, # Mk 14:54 one of the servant girls of the high priest came. 67When she saw Peter warming himself, she looked at him and said, “You also were with Jesus of Nazareth.”
68But he denied it, saying, “I neither know nor understand what you are saying.” Then he went out to the porch, and a cock crowed.
69The servant girl saw him again and began to say to those who stood by, “This man is one of them.” 70But again, he denied it.
A little while later, those who stood by said again to Peter, “Surely, you are one of them. For you are a Galilean, and your speech confirms it.” # Ac 2:7
71Peter began to invoke a curse on himself, and to swear, “I do not know this Man of whom you speak.”
72And the cock crowed a second time. And Peter called to mind the word that Jesus said to him, “Before the cock crows twice, you will deny Me three times.” # Mk 14:30, 68 And when he thought on this, he wept.
