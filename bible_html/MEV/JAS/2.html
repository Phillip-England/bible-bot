James 2

2
Warning Against Partiality
1My brothers, have faith in our Lord Jesus Christ, the Lord of glory, # 1Co 2:8 without partiality. # Lev 19:15 2For if a man with a gold ring, in fine clothing, comes into your assembly, and also a poor man in ragged clothing comes in, 3and you have respect for him who wears the fine clothing and say to him, “Sit here in a good place,” and say to the poor, “Stand there,” or “Sit here under my footstool,” 4have you not then become partial among yourselves and become judges with evil thoughts? # Jn 7:24
5Listen, my beloved brothers. Has God not chosen the poor of this world to be rich in faith # Lk 12:21 and heirs of the kingdom # Mt 5:3; Lk 6:20 which He has promised to those who love Him? 6But you have despised the poor. # 1Co 11:22 Do not rich men oppress you and drag you before the judgment seats? # Ac 8:3 7Do they not blaspheme that worthy name by which you are called? # Ac 11:26
8If you fulfill the royal law according to the Scripture, “You shall love your neighbor as yourself,” # Lev 19:18 # 2:8 Lev 19:18. you are doing well. 9But if you show partiality, you are committing sin and are convicted by the law as sinners. 10For whoever shall keep the whole law and yet offend in one point is guilty of breaking the whole law. # Gal 3:10 11For He who said, “Do not commit adultery,” # 2:11 Ex 20:14; Dt 5:18. also said, “Do not murder.” # Ex 20:13–14; Dt 5:17–18 # 2:11 Ex 20:13; Dt 5:17. Now if you do not commit adultery, yet you murder, you have become a lawbreaker.
12So speak and so do as those who will be judged by the law of liberty. # Jas 1:25 13For he who has shown no mercy will have judgment without mercy, # Pr 21:13 for mercy triumphs over judgment. # Mt 5:7
Faith and Works
14What does it profit, my brothers, if a man says he has faith but has no works? # Gal 5:6; Jas 1:22–25 Can faith save him? 15If a brother or sister is naked and lacking daily food, # Lk 3:11 16and one of you says to them, “Depart in peace, be warmed and filled,” and yet you give them nothing that the body needs, what does it profit? 17So faith by itself, if it has no works, is dead. # Jas 2:14, 26
18But a man may say, “You have faith and I have works.”
Show me your faith without your works, and I will show you my faith by my works. # Gal 5:6; Jas 3:13 19You believe that there is one God; # Dt 6:4 you do well. The demons also believe and tremble. # Mt 8:29
20But do you want to be shown, O foolish man, that faith without works is dead? # Jas 2:26 21Was not Abraham our father justified by works when he offered his son Isaac on the altar? # Ge 22:16–18 22Do you see how faith worked with his works, and by works faith was made complete? # 1Th 1:3 23The Scripture was fulfilled which says, “Abraham believed God, and it was reckoned to him as righteousness,” # Ge 15:6 # 2:23 Ge 15:6. and he was called the friend of God. # 2Ch 20:7 24You see then how by works a man is justified, and not by faith only.
25Likewise, was not Rahab the prostitute justified by works when she received the messengers and sent them out another way? # Jos 2:1, 19–21; Heb 11:31 26As the body without the spirit is dead, so faith without works is dead. # Jas 2:17, 20
