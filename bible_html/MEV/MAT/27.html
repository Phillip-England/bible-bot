Matthew 27

27
Jesus Brought Before Pilate
Mk 15:1; Lk 23:1–2; Jn 18:28–32
1When the morning came, all the chief priests and elders of the people took counsel against Jesus to put Him to death. # Mk 15:1; Lk 22:66 2When they had bound Him, they led Him away and handed Him over to Pontius Pilate the governor. # Mt 20:19; Ac 3:13
The Death of Judas
Ac 1:18–19
3When Judas, who had betrayed Him, saw that He was condemned, he repented and brought back the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests and elders, 4saying, “I have sinned in that I have betrayed innocent blood.”
They said, “What is that to us? You must see to that.”
5So he threw down the pieces of silver in the temple and departed. And he went and hanged himself. # 2Sa 17:23
6The chief priests took the pieces of silver and said, “It is not lawful to put them into the treasury, because this is the price of blood.” 7So they took counsel, and bought with them the potter’s field to bury strangers in. 8Therefore that field has been called the Field of Blood to this day. # Mt 28:15; Ac 1:19 9Then what was spoken by Jeremiah the prophet was fulfilled: “They took the thirty pieces of silver, # Jer 32:6–9; Zec 11:12–13 the price of Him on whom a value had been set by some of the Israelites, 10and they gave them for the potter’s field, as the Lord commanded me.” # 27:9–10 Zec 11:12–13; Jer 32:6–9.
Jesus Questioned by Pilate
Mk 15:2–5; Lk 23:3–5; Jn 18:33–38
11Jesus stood before the governor. And the governor asked Him, “Are You the King of the Jews?” # Mt 2:2
Jesus said to him, “You have said so.” # Mt 26:25
12When He was accused by the chief priests and elders, He gave no answer. # Mt 26:63 13Then Pilate said to Him, “Do You not hear how many things they testify against You?” # Mt 26:62 14But He never answered him a word, so that the governor was greatly amazed.
Jesus Sentenced to Die
Mk 15:6–15; Lk 23:13–25; Jn 18:39–19:16
15Now at the feast, the governor was accustomed to releasing to the people a prisoner whom they chose. 16They had then a notorious prisoner called Barabbas. 17So when they had gathered together, Pilate said to them, “Whom do you want me to release to you—Barabbas, or Jesus who is called Christ?” 18For he knew that they had handed Him over out of envy. # Pr 27:4
19When he was sitting on the judgment seat, his wife sent word to him, “Have nothing to do with that righteous Man, # Mt 27:24; Lk 23:47 for I have suffered much today in a dream on account of Him.”
20But the chief priests and elders persuaded the crowd to ask for Barabbas and kill Jesus.
21The governor answered, “Which of the two do you want me to release to you?”
They said, “Barabbas.”
22Pilate said to them, “Then what shall I do with Jesus who is called Christ?”
They all said to him, “Let Him be crucified!”
23The governor said, “Why, what evil has He done?”
But they cried out all the more, “Let Him be crucified!”
24When Pilate saw that he could not prevail, but rather that unrest was beginning, he took water and washed his hands before the crowd, saying, “I am innocent of the blood # Ps 26:6; Mt 27:4 of this righteous Man. See to it yourselves.”
25Then all the people answered, “His blood be on us and on our children!” # Jos 2:19; Ac 5:28
26Then he released Barabbas to them. But when he had scourged Jesus, # Isa 53:5; Jn 19:1 he handed Him over to be crucified.
The Soldiers Mock Jesus
Mk 15:16–20; Jn 19:2–3
27Then the soldiers of the governor took Jesus into the Praetorium, # Jn 18:28, 33 and gathered the whole detachment of soldiers before Him. 28They stripped Him and put a scarlet robe on Him, # Mk 15:17; Lk 23:11 29and when they wove a crown of thorns, they put it on His head and put a staff in His right hand. They knelt before Him and mocked Him, saying, “Hail, King of the Jews!” # Isa 53:3; Mt 20:19 30They spit on Him, and took the staff and hit Him on the head. # Mt 26:67; Mk 15:19 31After they had mocked Him, they took the robe off Him, put His own garments on Him, and led Him away to crucify Him. # Isa 53:7
The Crucifixion
Mk 15:21–32; Lk 23:26–43; Jn 19:17–27
32As they came out, they found a man of Cyrene, Simon by name. This man they compelled to bear His cross. # Mk 15:21; Lk 23:26 33When they came to a place called Golgotha, which means The Place of the Skull, 34they gave Him sour wine mingled with gall to drink. # Ps 69:21; Mt 27:48 But when He tasted it, He would not drink it. 35When they crucified Him, they divided His garments by casting lots to fulfill what was spoken by the prophet, “They divided My garments among themselves and for My clothing they cast lots.” # Ps 22:18 # 27:35 Ps 22:18. 36And sitting down, they kept watch over Him there. # Mt 27:54 37They put His accusation over His head, which read:
this is jesus the king of the jews. # Mk 15:26; Lk 23:38
38Then two thieves were crucified with Him, # Isa 53:12 one on the right and another on the left. 39Those who passed by insulted Him, shaking their heads, # Ps 109:25 40saying, “You who would destroy the temple and build it in three days, # Mt 26:61 save Yourself! If You are the Son of God, # Mt 4:3 come down from the cross.” 41Likewise the chief priests, with the scribes and elders, mocked Him, saying, 42“He saved others. He cannot save Himself. If He is the King of Israel, # Mt 27:37; Jn 1:49 let Him now come down from the cross, and we will believe Him. 43He trusted in God. Let Him deliver Him now, # Ps 22:8 if He will have Him. For He said, ‘I am the Son of God.’  ” 44Even the thieves who were crucified with Him insulted Him in the same way.
The Death of Jesus
Mk 15:33–41; Lk 23:44–49; Jn 19:28–30
45Now from the sixth hour until the ninth hour there was darkness over all the land. # Am 8:9; Mk 15:33–34 46About the ninth hour Jesus cried out with a loud voice, # Heb 5:7 “Eli, Eli, lama sabachthani?” which means, “My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?” # Ps 22:1 # 27:46 Ps 22:1.
47Some of those who stood there heard it and said, “This Man is calling for Elijah.”
48Immediately one of them ran, took a sponge, filled it with wine, and put it on a stick, and gave it to Him to drink. # Ps 69:21 49The rest said, “Leave Him alone. Let us see if Elijah will come to save Him.”
50And Jesus, when He had cried out again with a loud voice, released His spirit. # Mk 15:37; Lk 23:46; Jn 19:30
51And behold, the curtain of the temple was torn in two, # Ex 26:31–37; Mk 15:38 from the top to the bottom. And the ground shook, and the rocks split apart. 52The graves also were opened, and many bodies of the saints who had died were raised, # 1Co 15:20; 1Th 4:14 53and coming out of the graves after His resurrection, they went into the Holy City # Mt 4:5 and appeared to many.
54When the centurion and those with him, keeping watch over Jesus, # Mt 27:36 saw the earthquake and what took place, they feared greatly and said, “Truly He was the Son of God!” # Mt 27:43
55Many women who were there watching from afar followed Jesus from Galilee, serving Him, # Lk 8:2–3 56among whom was Mary Magdalene, # Mt 28:1; Jn 19:25 and Mary the mother of James and Joseph, and the mother of Zebedee’s sons.
The Burial of Jesus
Mk 15:42–47; Lk 23:50–56; Jn 19:38–42
57When the evening came, there came a rich man of Arimathea, named Joseph, who also was a disciple of Jesus. 58He went to Pilate and asked for the body of Jesus. Then Pilate commanded the body to be given to him. 59When Joseph had taken the body, he wrapped it in a clean linen cloth, 60and laid it in his own new tomb, which he had cut out of the rock. And he rolled a large stone to the door of the tomb and departed. # Mt 27:66; 28:2 61Mary Magdalene and the other Mary were there, # Mt 27:56; 28:1 sitting opposite the tomb.
The Guard at the Tomb
62The next day, following the Day of Preparation, # Mk 15:42; Jn 19:14 the chief priests and Pharisees gathered before Pilate, 63saying, “Sir, we remember that deceiver saying while He was still alive, ‘After three days I will rise.’ # Mt 16:21; 17:23 64Therefore command that the tomb be made secure until the third day, lest His disciples come by night and steal Him away, # Mt 28:13 and tell the people, ‘He has risen from the dead.’ The last deception will be worse than the first.”
65Pilate said to them, “You have a guard. Go your way. Make it as secure as you can.” 66So they went and made the tomb secure by sealing the stone and posting the guard. # Da 6:17
