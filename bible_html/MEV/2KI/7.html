2 Kings 7

7
1Then Elisha said, “Hear the word of the Lord: Thus says the Lord: Tomorrow about this time one measure # 7:1 Likely about 12 pounds, or 5.5 kilograms of flour, and in vv. 16 and 18. of fine flour will be sold for one shekel, # 7:1 About 2/5 ounce, or 12 grams, and in vv. 16 and 18. and two measures # 7:1 Likely about 20 pounds, or 9 kilograms of barley, and in vv. 16 and 18. of barley for one shekel, at the gate of Samaria.”
2Then an officer on whose hand the king leaned # 2Ki 5:18 answered the man of God, “If the Lord were to make windows in heaven, # Ge 7:11; Mal 3:10 could this thing happen?”
And he said, “You will see it with your eyes, but you will not eat from it.”
The Arameans Flee
3There were four leprous men at the entry of the gate, # Nu 5:2–4; 12:14 and they said to one another, “Why are we sitting here until we die? 4If we say, ‘Let us enter the city,’ the famine is in the city, and we shall die there. But if we sit here, we die also. Now come, let us fall into the camp of the Arameans. If they spare our lives, we will live, and if they kill us, we will die.”
5So they rose at twilight to enter the camp of the Arameans. When they came to the edge of the camp of the Arameans, behold, there was no one there. 6For the Lord had caused the Aramean camp to hear the sound of chariots, horses, even the sound of a large army, # 2Sa 5:24; 1Ki 10:29 so that they said to one another, “Behold, the king of Israel has hired the kings of the Hittites and the kings of the Egyptians to come against us.” 7So they got up and ran away in the twilight and abandoned their tents, their horses, and their donkeys. The camp remained just as it was, and they ran for their lives. # Ps 48:4–6; Pr 28:1
8When these leprous men came to the edge of the camp, they went into one tent. They ate and drank, carried off silver, gold, and clothes, and went and hid them. # Jos 7:21 Then they went back, entered another tent, and carried off things from there and went and hid them.
9Then they said to one another, “We are not doing right today. This is a day of good news. If we are silent and wait until the morning light, we will be found guilty. Let us go now and enter the city and tell the king’s household.”
10So they went and called to the gatekeepers of the city, and they told them, “We came to the camp of the Arameans, and behold, there was no one there. There was no sound of a man’s voice, only horses tied, donkeys tied, and the tents as they were.” 11Then the gatekeepers called out and told the king’s household inside.
12The king got up in the night and said to his servants, “I will show you what the Arameans have done to us. They know that we are starving, so they left the camp to hide themselves in the field, saying, ‘When they come out of the city, we will capture them alive and get into the city.’ ”
13One of his servants answered, “Let some men take five of the remaining horses, since those remaining will suffer the fate of the whole multitude of Israel that have perished already; so let us send them and see.”
14So they took two chariots with horses, and the king sent them after the army of the Arameans, saying, “Go and see.” 15So they went after them to the Jordan, and behold, the whole way was full of clothes and vessels, which the Arameans had thrown away in their haste. Then the messengers returned and told the king. 16Then the people went out and looted the camp # Isa 33:4 of the Arameans. So one measure of fine flour was sold for one shekel, and two measures of barley for one shekel, according to the word of the Lord. # 2Ki 7:1
17The king had appointed the officer on whose hand he leaned to have charge over the gate, and the people trampled him in the gate, and he died, just as the man of God had said when the king came down to him. # 2Ki 6:32; 7:2 18Then the man of God had spoken to the king, saying, “Two seahs of barley shall be sold for one shekel, and one seah of fine flour for one shekel, about this time tomorrow at the gate of Samaria.”
19The officer had answered the man of God, “If the Lord should make windows in heaven, could such a thing happen?” And he had said, “You shall see it with your own eyes, but you shall not eat of it.” # 2Ki 7:2 20So it happened to him, for the people trampled him in the gate, and he died.
