Numbers 14

14
Israel Refuses to Enter Canaan
1And the whole assembly lifted up their voices and cried, and the people wept that night. 2All the Israelites grumbled against Moses and against Aaron, # Ex 15:24; 17:3; Ps 106:25; Jn 7:12, 32; Php 2:14 and the whole assembly said to them, “O that we had died in the land of Egypt! Or that we had died in this wilderness! 3And why has the Lord brought us to this land to fall by the sword, that our wives and our children should become prey? Is it not better for us to return to Egypt?” 4And they said one to another, “Let us select a leader, and let us return to Egypt.” # Dt 17:16; Ac 7:39
5Then Moses and Aaron fell on their faces # Nu 16:4, 22 before all the congregation of the assembly of the Israelites. 6Joshua the son of Nun, and Caleb the son of Jephunneh, # Nu 13:6, 8 from the ones who explored the land, tore their clothes. 7And they spoke to all the assembly of the Israelites, saying, “The land which we passed through to explore it is a very, very good land. # Nu 13:27; Dt 1:25 8If the Lord delights in us, # Dt 10:15 then He will bring us into this land and give it to us, a land which flows with milk and honey. # Nu 13:27 9Only do not rebel against the Lord, # Dt 9:23–24 nor fear the people of the land because they are bread for us. Their defense is gone from them, and the Lord is with us. Do not fear them.” # Dt 7:18
10But all the assembly said, “Stone them with stones.” # Ex 17:4 And the glory of the Lord appeared at the tent of meeting before all the Israelites. # Lev 9:23 11The Lord said to Moses, “How long will this people disgrace Me? And how long will they not believe Me, # Ps 78:22; 106:24 in spite of all the signs which I have done among them? 12I will strike them with the pestilence, and disinherit them, and will make from you a nation greater and mightier than they.” # Ex 32:10
Moses Intercedes for the People
13Moses said to the Lord, “Then the Egyptians will hear of it, for by Your power You brought this people up from among them, 14and they will say to the inhabitants of this land that they heard that You, # Ex 15:14 Lord, are among this people, that You, Lord, are seen face to face, # Ge 32:30 and Your cloud stands over them, and in the pillar of cloud You go before them by day and by a pillar of fire at night. 15Now if You kill all this people as one man, then the nations which have heard the fame of You will speak, saying, 16‘The Lord was not able to bring this people into the land which He swore to them, so He slaughtered them in the wilderness.’ # Dt 9:28; Jos 7:9
17“So now, I pray, let the power of my Lord be great, just as You have spoken, saying, 18‘The Lord is slow to anger and abounding in mercy, forgiving iniquity and transgression, but He will by no means clear the guilty, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children to the third and fourth generation.’ # Ex 20:5; 34:6–7; Ps 145:8; Jon 4:2; Na 1:2–3 19Pardon the iniquity of this people, # Ex 34:9 I pray, according to the greatness of Your grace, just as You have pardoned this people, from Egypt even until now.” # Ps 78:38
20The Lord said, “I have forgiven according to your word. 21But truly as I live, all the earth will be filled with the glory of the Lord. # Hab 2:14 22Because all those men seeing My glory and My signs which I did in Egypt and in the wilderness, and have tempted Me now these ten times, # Ge 31:7; Ex 17:2 and have not listened to My voice, 23surely they will not see the land which I swore to their fathers, nor will any of them who disgraced Me see it. # Nu 26:64; 32:11 24But My servant Caleb, # Nu 14:6–9; 26:65 because he had a different spirit with him and followed Me fully, I will bring him into the land where he went, and his seed will possess it. 25Now the Amalekites and the Canaanites lived in the valley. # Nu 13:29 Tomorrow you will turn, and you will set out for the wilderness by the way of the Red Sea.” # Dt 1:40
Death of Rebels Predicted
26The Lord spoke to Moses and to Aaron, saying: 27How long will this evil assembly be murmuring against Me? I have heard the murmurings of the Israelites which they murmur against Me. # Ex 16:12 28Say to them, “As I live,” says the Lord, “just as you have spoken in My ears, so I will do to you. # Nu 14:21, 23 29In this wilderness your corpses will fall, # Heb 3:17 and all who were numbered of you, according to your whole number, from twenty years old and upward, # Nu 1:45 who have murmured against Me, 30you will not go into the land which I swore by My hand to cause you to dwell in it, # Heb 3:7–11 except Caleb the son of Jephunneh and Joshua the son of Nun. # Nu 14:38 31But your children, whom you said would be a prey, # Dt 1:39 I will bring them in and they will know the land, which you rejected. # Ps 106:24 32But as for you, your corpses will fall in this wilderness. # 1Co 10:5 33Your children will be shepherds in the wilderness forty years, # Nu 32:13 and they will suffer for your fornications, until your corpses are in the wilderness. # Dt 2:14 34According to the number of the days in which you spied out the land, forty days, # Nu 13:25 a year for each day you will bear your iniquity, forty years, # Eze 4:6 and you will know My displeasure.” 35I the Lord have spoken. # Nu 23:19 I will surely do this to all this evil assembly gathered against Me. In this wilderness they will be finished, and there they will die.
36The men whom Moses sent to explore the land, who returned and made all the assembly murmur against him by bringing up an evil report about the land, 37the men who brought an evil report about the land, died by the pestilence before the Lord. # Nu 16:49; 1Co 10:10 38But Joshua the son of Nun and Caleb the son of Jephunneh, from the men that went to explore the land, lived. # Nu 26:65
Defeat in Battle
39Moses spoke these words to all the Israelites, and the people mourned greatly. # Ex 33:4 40They rose up early in the morning, and they went up to the top of the mountain, saying, “Here we are. We will go up to the place which the Lord has promised, for we have sinned.” # Dt 1:41
41Moses said, “Why do you now transgress the commandment of the Lord? # 2Ch 24:20 This will not prosper. 42Do not go up, for the Lord is not among you; do not let yourselves be defeated by your enemies. # Dt 1:42 43For the Amalekites and the Canaanites are there before you, # Nu 13:29; 14:25 and you will fall by the sword because you are turned from the Lord, and the Lord will not be with you.”
44But they presumed to go up to the top of the mountain. # Dt 1:43 But the ark of the covenant of the Lord and Moses did not depart from the midst of the camp. 45Then the Amalekites and the Canaanites who dwelt on that mountain went down and defeated them, pursuing them as far as Hormah. # Nu 21:3; Dt 1:44
