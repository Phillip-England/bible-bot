Acts 20

20
Paul’s Journey to Macedonia and Greece
1After the uproar ceased, Paul summoned the disciples and embraced them and departed for Macedonia. # Ac 19:21 2When he had gone through that region and had greatly exhorted them, he came to Greece, 3and stayed there three months. When the Jews plotted against him # Ac 9:23–24; 20:19 as he was about to sail to Syria, he decided to return through Macedonia. 4Accompanying him to Asia were Sopater of Berea, and Aristarchus # Ac 19:29 and Secundus of Thessalonica, Gaius of Derbe, and Timothy, # Ac 16:1 and Tychicus # Eph 6:21 and Trophimus # Ac 21:29 of Asia. 5These men went forward and waited for us at Troas. # Ac 16:8, 10–11 6But we sailed away from Philippi # Ac 12:3; 16:12 after the Days of Unleavened Bread, and after five days we came to them at Troas, where we stayed for seven days.
Paul’s Farewell Visit to Troas
7On the first day of the week, # 1Co 16:2; Rev 1:10 when the disciples came together to break bread, # Ac 20:11 Paul, ready to leave the next day, preached to them and continued his message until midnight. 8There were many lamps in the upper room # Ac 1:13 where they were assembled. 9A young man named Eutychus sat in the window, falling into a deep sleep as Paul spoke for a longer time. Being overcome by sleep, he fell down from the third floor and was taken up dead. 10Paul went down and leaned over him, and embracing him said, “Do not be troubled, for he is alive.” # Mt 9:23–24; Mk 5:39 11When he had gone up and had broken bread and eaten, # Ac 20:7 he conversed for a long while until dawn and departed. 12They took the lad in alive and were greatly comforted.
Paul’s Voyage from Troas to Miletus
13We went ahead to the ship and sailed to Assos, intending to take Paul on board there. For he had arranged this, intending to go on foot. 14When he met us at Assos, we took him on board and went to Mitylene. 15We sailed from there, and on the next day arrived off Chios. The next day we crossed over to Samos and stayed at Trogyllium, and the following day we came to Miletus. # Ac 20:17; 2Ti 4:20 16Paul had decided to sail by Ephesus, to avoid spending time in Asia. For he was hurrying so he could be in Jerusalem, if possible, on the day of Pentecost. # Ac 2:1; 19:21
Paul Speaks to the Ephesian Elders
17From Miletus he sent to Ephesus for the elders of the church. # Ac 11:30; 14:23 18When they came to him, he said to them, “You know how I always lived among you from the first day that I came to Asia, # Ac 18:19; 19:1 19serving the Lord with all humility and with many tears and trials which befell me through the plots of the Jews. # Ac 20:3, 31 20I did not keep from declaring what was beneficial to you, # Ac 20:27 and teaching you publicly and from house to house, 21testifying to both Jews and Greeks of repentance toward God and of faith in our Lord Jesus Christ. # Ac 2:38; 11:18; 20:24
22“Now, behold, having been bound by the Spirit, I am going to Jerusalem, not knowing what will happen to me there, 23except that the Holy Spirit testifies to me in every city that imprisonment and afflictions await me. # Ac 9:16; 21:4 24But none of these things deter me. Nor do I count my life of value to myself, so that I may joyfully finish my course and the ministry which I have received from the Lord Jesus, to testify to the gospel of the grace of God. # Ac 21:13; 2Co 4:1
25“Now, behold, I know that you all, among whom I went proclaiming the kingdom of God, # Mt 4:23; Ac 28:31 will see my face no more. 26Therefore I testify to you this day that I am innocent of the blood of all men. # Ac 18:6 27For I did not keep from declaring to you the whole counsel of God. # Lk 7:30; Ac 20:20 28Therefore take heed to yourselves and to the entire flock, over which the Holy Spirit has made you overseers, to shepherd the church of God # Isa 40:11; 1Co 10:32 which He purchased with His own blood. 29For I know this, that after my departure savage wolves will enter among you, not sparing the flock. # Mt 7:15; Jn 10:12 30Even from among you men will arise speaking perverse things, # 1Ti 1:19–20 to draw the disciples away after them. # 1Jn 2:19 31Therefore watch, remembering that for three years night and day # Ac 19:8, 10 I did not cease to warn everyone with tears.
32“Now, brothers, I commend you to God # Ac 14:23 and to the word of His grace, which is able to build you up and to give you an inheritance among all who are sanctified. # Ac 26:18 33I have not coveted anyone’s silver or gold or clothing. # 1Co 9:12; 2Co 11:9 34Yes, you yourselves know that these hands have provided for my necessities and for those who were with me. # Ac 18:3 35In all things I have shown you how, working like this, you must help the weak, remembering the words of the Lord Jesus, how He said, ‘It is more blessed to give than to receive.’  ” # Mt 10:8
36Having said these things, he knelt down with all of them and prayed. # Lk 22:41; Ac 21:5 37They all wept much and embraced Paul’s neck and kissed him, # Lk 15:20; Ro 16:16; 2Ti 1:4 38grieving most over the words he spoke, that they were to see his face no more. # Ac 20:25 Then they escorted him to the ship.
