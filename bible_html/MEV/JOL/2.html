Joel 2

2
The Day of the Lord
1Blow the ram’s horn in Zion, # Joel 2:15
sound the alarm on My holy mountain!
All the inhabitants of the earth will tremble,
because the day of the Lord has come, # Joel 1:15
because it is near—
2a day of darkness and gloom,
a day of clouds and thick darkness.
Like blackness spreading over the mountain,
a great and mighty army comes, # Joel 1:6; 2:25
such as was never before,
and will never be again,
even through the years of all generations.
3Before them fire devours,
and behind them a flame blazes;
the land is like the garden of Eden before them, # Ge 2:8; Isa 51:3
but behind them a desolate wasteland,
and nothing escapes them.
4They have the appearance of horses, # Rev 9:7
and like horsemen they run.
5As with the sound of chariots, # Rev 9:9
they leap on the mountaintops,
as with the sound of a flame of fire consuming stubble, # Isa 5:24
as a mighty army arrayed for war.
6Before them, peoples are tormented;
every face turns pale. # Isa 13:8; Na 2:10
7Like mighty men they run, # Pr 30:27
like men of war they scale a wall;
each marches on his way,
they do not swerve from their paths.
8They do not jostle one another;
each marches in his track.
Through the weapons they plunge;
they do not break rank.
9They rush on the city,
they run on the wall;
they climb up into the houses,
they enter through the windows like thieves. # Jer 9:21; Jn 10:1
10Before them the earth quakes, # Ps 18:7
the heavens shake;
the sun and moon darken,
and the stars withdraw their radiance. # Isa 13:10
11The Lord has sounded His voice # Joel 3:16
before His army,
for His camp is exceedingly great;
mighty is the one who accomplishes His word.
For great is the day of the Lord, # Rev 18:8
and very awe-inspiring.
Who can endure it?
Return to the Lord
12Yet even now, declares the Lord,
return to Me with all your heart, # Jer 4:1; Hos 12:6
and with fasting and with weeping and with mourning.
13Rend your heart, # Ps 34:18
and not your garments;
return to the Lord your God,
for He is gracious and merciful,
slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love;
and He relents from punishing. # Ex 34:6; Jon 4:2; Na 1:3
14Who knows? He might turn aside and relent,
and He might leave behind a blessing # Hag 2:19 —
a grain offering and a food offering # Joel 1:13
for the Lord your God.
15Blow the ram’s horn in Zion,
consecrate a fast,
call a sacred assembly. # Nu 10:3; Joel 1:14
16Gather the people,
consecrate the congregation, # Ex 19:10, 22
assemble the elders,
gather the children
and those nursing at the breast;
let the bridegroom leave his room
and the bride her chamber.
17Between the temple porch and the altar, # Eze 8:16
let the priests, ministers of the Lord, weep and say,
“Have pity upon Your people,
and do not make Your inheritance a disgrace,
a mockery among the nations.
Why should they say among the peoples,
‘Where is their God?’ ” # Ps 79:10
Rescue and Restitution
18Then the Lord became jealous for His land
and took pity upon His people. # Zec 1:14; 8:2
19So the Lord answered and said to His people,
Behold, I am sending you grain, new wine, and oil, # Joel 1:10
and you will be satisfied,
and I will never again make you
a disgrace among the nations. # Eze 34:29
20I will remove the northerner far from you,
and I will banish him to a dry and desolate land,
those in front to the eastern sea,
and those in back to the western sea, # Dt 11:24; Zec 14:8
and his stink will rise,
and his stench will rise, # Isa 34:3
for he has done great things.
21Do not be afraid, land; # Isa 54:4; Zep 3:16–17
exult and rejoice,
for the Lord has done great things!
22Do not be afraid, beasts of the field,
because the wild pastures flourish,
because the tree bears its fruit;
the fig tree and the vine yield their abundance. # Ps 65:12; Joel 1:18–20
23And children of Zion, exult
and rejoice in the Lord your God, # Zec 10:7
because He has given to you
the early rain for vindication.
He showers down rains for you,
the early rain and the latter rain, as before. # Lev 26:4
24Then the threshing floors will be filled with grain,
and the vats will overflow with new wine and oil. # Lev 26:10; Am 9:13
25And I will compensate you for the years the locusts have eaten—
the larval locust, the hopper locust,
and the fledging locust—
My great army which I sent against you. # Joel 1:4–7; 2:2–11
26You will eat abundantly and be satisfied, # Lev 26:5
and you will praise the name of the Lord your God,
who has worked wonders for you; # Isa 25:1
and My people will never again be shamed.
27Then you will know that I am in the midst of Israel,
that I am the Lord your God, # Lev 26:11–12; Joel 3:17
and that there is no other.
And My people will never again be shamed.
The Outpouring of the Spirit
28And it will be that, afterwards,
I will pour out My Spirit on all flesh; # Isa 32:15; Eze 39:29
then your sons and your daughters will prophesy,
your old men will dream dreams,
and your young men will see visions.
29Even on the menservants and maidservants
in those days I will pour out My Spirit. # 1Co 12:13; Gal 3:28
30Then I will work wonders in the heavens
and the earth—
blood and fire and columns of smoke. # Mt 24:29; Lk 21:11
31The sun will be turned to darkness,
and the moon to blood, # Isa 13:9–10; Rev 6:12
before the great and awe-inspiring day of the Lord comes. # Mal 4:5
32And it will be that everyone
who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved.
For on Mount Zion and in Jerusalem
there will be deliverance, # Isa 46:13; Ob 17
as the Lord has said,
and among the survivors
whom the Lord calls.
