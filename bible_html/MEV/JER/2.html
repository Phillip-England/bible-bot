Jeremiah 2

2
God Pleads with Israel to Repent
1Moreover the word of the Lord came to me, saying, 2Go and proclaim in the ears of Jerusalem, saying,
Thus says the Lord:
I remember you, the kindness of your youth, # Eze 16:8
the love of your espousals,
when you followed Me in the wilderness, # Dt 2:7
in a land that was not sown.
3Israel is holy to the Lord, # Ex 19:5–6
and the firstfruits of His harvest. # Jas 1:18
All who eat of it will become guilty;
disaster will come upon them,
says the Lord.
4Hear the word of the Lord, O house of Jacob
and all the families of the house of Israel.
5Thus says the Lord:
What iniquity have your fathers found in Me,
that they have gone far from Me, # Jer 2:31
and have walked after vanity
and have become vain? # 2Ki 17:15
6They did not say, “Where is the Lord
who brought us up out of the land of Egypt, # Hos 13:4
who led us through the wilderness,
through a land of deserts and of pits, # Dt 32:10
through a land of drought and of the shadow of death,
through a land that no man passed through and where no man lived?”
7And I brought you into a plentiful country
to eat its fruit and its goodness. # Dt 8:7–9
But when you entered, you defiled My land
and made My inheritance an abomination. # Jer 16:18
8The priests did not say,
“Where is the Lord?”
And those who handle the law did not know Me;
the shepherds also transgressed against Me,
and the prophets prophesied by Baal
and walked after things that do not profit. # Jer 5:31; Hab 2:18
9Therefore I will yet contend with you,
says the Lord,
and with your sons’ sons I will contend. # Jer 2:35; Eze 20:35–36
10For pass over the coastlands of Cyprus and see,
and send to Kedar # Ps 120:5 and consider diligently,
and see if there is such a thing.
11Has a nation changed their gods,
though they are not gods? # Isa 37:19
But My people have changed their glory
for that which does not profit. # Ps 106:20
12Be astonished at this, O heavens, # Isa 1:2
and be horribly afraid, be very desolate,
says the Lord.
13For My people have committed two evils.
They have forsaken Me,
the fountain of living waters, # Ps 36:9; Jer 17:13
and hewed out for themselves cisterns,
broken cisterns, that can hold no water.
14Is Israel a servant? Is he a homeborn slave? # Ex 4:22
Why has he been plundered?
15The young lions roared at him # Jer 4:7; 50:17
and roared loudly,
and they made his land a waste;
his cities have been destroyed, without inhabitant.
16Also the men of Memphis and Tahpanhes # Jer 46:14
have shaved the crown of your head. # Dt 33:20
17Have you not brought this on yourself, # Jer 4:18
in that you have forsaken the Lord your God
when He led you in the way?
18And now what are you doing in the way of Egypt, # Jos 13:3; Isa 31:1
to drink the waters of the Nile?
Or what are you doing in the way of Assyria,
to drink the waters of the River?
19Your own wickedness will correct you,
and your backslidings will reprove you. # Isa 3:9; Hos 5:5
Know therefore and see
that it is an evil thing and bitter for you
to have forsaken the Lord your God,
and the fear of Me is not in you,
says the Lord God of Hosts.
20For long ago I have broken your yoke
and burst your bonds; # Lev 26:13
and you said, “I will not serve.”
For upon every high hill
and under every green tree # Dt 12:2
you wander, playing the harlot. # Isa 1:21
21Yet I had planted you a noble vine, # Ex 15:17; Ps 80:8
a wholly faithful seed.
How then have you turned
into the degenerate plant of a strange vine to Me?
22For though you wash yourself with lye,
and take much soap,
yet your iniquity is marked before Me, # Job 14:17; Jer 17:1
says the Lord God.
23How can you say, “I am not polluted, # Pr 30:12
I have not gone after the Baals”?
See your way in the valley. # Jer 7:31
Know what you have done.
You are a swift young camel
entangling her ways,
24a wild donkey used to the wilderness # Jer 14:6
that sniffs the wind in her passion.
In the time of her heat, who can turn her away?
All those who seek her will not weary themselves;
in her month they shall find her.
25Withhold your foot from being unshod
and your throat from thirst.
But you said, “There is no hope. No!
For I have loved strangers, # Dt 32:16; Jer 3:13
and after them I will go.”
26As the thief is ashamed when he is found,
so is the house of Israel ashamed.
They, their kings, their officials,
and their priests, and their prophets # Jer 32:32
27say to a tree, “You are my father.”
And to a stone, “You gave birth to me.”
For they have turned their back to Me,
and not their face.
But in the time of their trouble they will say,
“Arise and save us.” # Isa 26:16
28But where are your gods that you have made for yourself? # Dt 32:37
Let them arise, if they can save you # Isa 45:20
in the time of your trouble;
for according to the number of your cities
are your gods, O Judah.
29Why will you plead with Me?
You all have transgressed against Me, # Jer 5:1; 6:13
says the Lord.
30In vain I have smitten your children;
they received no correction.
Your own sword has devoured your prophets # Ne 9:26; 1Th 2:15
like a destroying lion.
31O generation, consider the word of the Lord:
Have I been a wilderness to Israel?
A land of darkness?
Why do My people say, “We are free to roam.
We will come no more to You”? # Dt 32:15
32Can a virgin forget her ornaments,
or a bride her attire?
Yet My people have forgotten Me # Ps 106:21; Isa 17:10
for days without number.
33You prepare your way very well to seek love.
Therefore you have also taught the wicked women your ways.
34Also in your skirts is found
the lifeblood of the innocent poor, # 2Ki 21:16; 24:4
although you never caught them breaking in.
Yet in spite of all these things,
35you say, “I am innocent;
surely His anger shall turn away from me.”
Behold, I will judge you,
because you say, “I have not sinned.” # Pr 28:13
36Why do you go about so much
to change your way? # Jer 31:22; Hos 5:13
You also shall be ashamed of Egypt,
as you were ashamed of Assyria.
37Indeed, you shall go forth from him
with your hands upon your head; # 2Sa 13:19
for the Lord has rejected those in whom you trust,
and you shall not prosper in them. # Jer 37:7–10
