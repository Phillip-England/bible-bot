Jeremiah 5

5
The Justice of God’s Judgment
1Run to and fro through the streets of Jerusalem,
see now and know;
seek in the open places
if you can find a man,
if there is any who executes justice, who seeks the truth, # 2Ch 16:9; Eze 22:30
that I may pardon her.
2Though they say, “The Lord lives,”
surely they swear falsely. # Isa 48:1; Titus 1:16
3O  Lord, are not Your eyes upon the truth?
You have stricken them, but they have not grieved.
You have consumed them, but they have refused to receive correction. # Jer 2:30
They have made their faces harder than a rock;
they have refused to return.
4Therefore I said, “Surely these are the poor.
They are foolish;
for they know not the way of the Lord
or the judgment of their God. # Jer 8:7
5I will go to the great men
and will speak to them,
for they have known the way of the Lord
and the judgment of their God.”
But these have altogether broken the yoke
and burst the bonds. # Jer 2:20
6Therefore a lion out of the forest will slay them, # Jer 4:7
and a wolf from the deserts will destroy them;
a leopard will watch over their cities. # Hab 1:8
Everyone who goes out from there will be torn in pieces,
because their transgressions are many
and their backslidings have increased.
7How shall I pardon you for this?
Your children have forsaken Me
and sworn by those who are not gods. # Dt 32:21; Jos 23:7
When I had fed them to the full,
then they committed adultery
and assembled themselves by troops in the harlots’ houses.
8They were as fed horses in the morning;
everyone neighed after his neighbor’s wife. # Jer 13:27; Eze 22:11
9Shall I not punish for these things? says the Lord,
and shall not My soul be avenged on such a nation as this? # Jer 5:29; 9:9
10Go up upon her walls and destroy, # Jer 39:8
but make not a full end. # Jer 4:27
Take away her battlements.
For they are not the Lord’s.
11For the house of Israel and the house of Judah
have dealt very treacherously against Me, # Jer 3:20
says the Lord.
12They have lied about the Lord,
and said, “Not He.
Neither shall evil come upon us,
nor shall we see sword or famine.” # 2Ch 36:16; Jer 43:2–3
13And the prophets shall become wind, # Job 8:2
and the word is not in them.
Thus it shall be done to them! # Jer 14:15
14Therefore thus says the Lord God of Hosts:
Because you speak this word,
behold, I will make My words in your mouth fire # Jer 1:9; 23:29
and this people wood, and it shall devour them.
15Behold, I will bring a nation upon you from far, # Dt 28:49
O house of Israel, says the Lord.
It is a mighty nation, it is an ancient nation,
a nation whose language you do not know,
nor do you understand what they say. # Isa 5:26
16Their quiver is as an open sepulcher, # Ps 5:9; Isa 5:28
they all are mighty men.
17They will eat up your harvest and your bread
which your sons and your daughters should eat.
They will eat up your flocks and your herds;
they will eat up your vines and your fig trees; # Lev 26:16; Dt 28:33
they will impoverish your fenced cities,
in which you trusted, with the sword.
18Nevertheless in those days, says the Lord, I will not make a full end of you. # Jer 4:27; 5:10 19It shall come to pass when they say, “Why does the Lord our God do all these things to us?” then you shall answer them, “As you have forsaken Me and served strange gods in your land, so you will serve strangers in a land that is not yours.” # 1Ki 9:8–9; Jer 13:22
20Declare this in the house of Jacob
and proclaim it in Judah, saying:
21Hear this now, O foolish people and without understanding,
who have eyes but do not see,
who have ears but do not hear. # Isa 6:9–10; Eze 12:2
22Do you not fear Me? says the Lord.
Will you not tremble at My presence? # Dt 28:58; Ps 119:120
For I have placed the sand for the boundary of the sea
by a perpetual decree so that it cannot pass over it.
And though the waves toss themselves, yet they cannot prevail;
though they roar, yet they cannot pass over it.
23But this people has a revolting and a rebellious heart;
they have revolted and gone aside. # Jer 6:28
24They do not say in their heart,
“Let us now fear the Lord our God,
who gives rain, both the former and the latter, in its season. # Ps 147:8
He reserves for us the appointed weeks of the harvest.” # Ge 8:22
25Your iniquities have turned away these things,
and your sins have withheld good things from you. # Jer 3:3
26For among My people are found wicked men.
They lie in wait, as he who sets snares;
they set a trap, they catch men. # Pr 1:11; Jer 18:22
27As a cage is full of birds,
so are their houses full of deceit.
Therefore they have become great and rich.
28They are fat, # Dt 32:15 they are sleek.
Indeed, they excel in deeds of wickedness.
They do not judge the cause of the fatherless, # Isa 1:23
so that they may prosper;
and the right of the needy they do not defend.
29Shall I not punish them for these things?
says the Lord.
Shall not My soul be avenged
on such a nation as this? # Jer 5:9; Mal 3:5
30An appalling and horrible thing
has been committed in the land. # Jer 23:14; Hos 6:10
31The prophets prophesy falsely,
and the priests rule by their own authority; # Eze 13:6; Mic 2:11
and My people love to have it so.
Yet what will you do in the end?
