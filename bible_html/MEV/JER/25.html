Jeremiah 25

25
Seventy Years of Captivity
1The word that came to Jeremiah concerning all the people of Judah in the fourth year of Jehoiakim the son of Josiah, king of Judah, # Jer 36:1 that was the first year of Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon, # 2Ki 24:1–2 2which Jeremiah the prophet spoke to all the people of Judah and to all the inhabitants of Jerusalem, # Jer 18:11 saying: 3From the thirteenth year of Josiah the son of Amon, king of Judah, # Jer 1:2 even to this day, these twenty-three years the word of the Lord has come to me, and I have spoken to you, rising early and speaking, # Jer 11:7 but you have not listened.
4The Lord has sent to you all His servants the prophets, rising early and sending them, but you have not listened nor inclined your ear to hear. # Jer 26:5 5They said, “Turn now again everyone from his evil way and from the evil of your deeds, and dwell in the land that the Lord has given to you and to your fathers forever and ever. # Ge 17:8; Jer 7:7 6And do not go after other gods to serve them and to worship them, # Dt 6:14; 8:19 and provoke Me not to anger with the works of your hands; and I will do you no harm.”
7Yet you have not listened to Me, says the Lord, that you might provoke Me to anger # Dt 32:21; 2Ki 21:15 with the works of your hands to your own harm.
8Therefore thus says the Lord of Hosts: Because you have not obeyed My words, 9behold, I will send and take all the families of the north, # Jer 1:15 says the Lord, and Nebuchadnezzar the king of Babylon, My servant, and will bring them against this land and its inhabitants, and against all these surrounding nations; I will utterly destroy them, and make them an astonishment, a hissing, and perpetual desolations. # Jer 18:16 10Moreover I will take from them the voice of laughter and the voice of gladness, # Eze 26:13 the voice of the bridegroom and the voice of the bride, # Jer 7:34 the sound of the millstones and the light of the candle. 11This whole land shall be a desolation and an astonishment, and these nations will serve the king of Babylon seventy years. # 2Ch 36:21–22; Da 9:2
12It shall come to pass when seventy years are finished # Jer 29:10 that I will punish the king of Babylon and that nation, says the Lord, for their iniquity, and the land of the Chaldeans, # Isa 13:19 and will make it perpetual desolations. 13I will bring upon that land all My words which I have pronounced against it, even all that is written in this book, which Jeremiah has prophesied against all the nations. # Jer 1:5, 10 14For many nations and great kings # Jer 27:7; 50:9 will make slaves of them, even themselves. And I will recompense them according to their deeds and according to the works of their hands. # Jer 51:6
The Cup of God’s Wrath
15For thus says the Lord God of Israel to me: Take the wine cup of this fury at My hand and cause all the nations to whom I send you to drink it. # Ps 75:8; Isa 51:17 16They will drink, # Jer 51:7; Na 3:11 and totter, and be mad, because of the sword that I will send among them.
17Then I took the cup from the hand of the Lord and made all the nations, to whom the Lord had sent me, drink: # Jer 25:28 18Jerusalem and the cities of Judah and the kings and the officials, to make them a desolation, an astonishment, a hissing, and a curse, # Ps 60:3; Jer 24:9 as it is this day; 19Pharaoh king of Egypt, # Jer 46:2, 13–26 and his servants, and his officials, and all his people; 20and all the foreign people, and all the kings of the land of Uz, # Job 1:1 and all the kings of the land of the Philistines (even Ashkelon, and Gaza, and Ekron, and the remnant of Ashdod); # Isa 20:1 21Edom, and Moab, # Ps 137:7; Jer 48:1–49:22 and the Ammonites; 22and all the kings of Tyre, and all the kings of Sidon, # Jer 47:4 and the kings of the coastlands which are beyond the sea; 23Dedan, # Jer 49:8 and Tema, and Buz, and all who cut the corners of their hair; # Jer 9:26 24and all the kings of Arabia, # 2Ch 9:14 and all the kings of the foreign people # Jer 25:20 who dwell in the desert; 25and all the kings of Zimri, and all the kings of Elam, # Ge 10:22; Isa 11:11 and all the kings of the Medes; 26and all the kings of the north, far and near, one with another; and all the kingdoms of the earth which are upon the face of the earth, and the king of Sheshak # Jer 51:41 shall drink after them.
27Therefore you shall say to them, Thus says the Lord of Hosts, the God of Israel: Drink and be drunk # Hab 2:16 and spew and fall and rise no more because of the sword which I will send among you. # Jer 25:16 28It will be, if they refuse to take the cup from your hand to drink, then you will say to them: Thus says the Lord of Hosts: You shall certainly drink! 29For behold, I begin to bring calamity on the city which is called by My name, and should you be utterly unpunished? You shall not be unpunished. # Pr 11:31 For I will call for a sword upon all the inhabitants of the earth, # Eze 38:21 says the Lord of Hosts.
30Therefore prophesy against them all these words, and say to them:
The Lord will roar from on high,
and utter His voice from His holy habitation; # Joel 3:16; Am 1:2
He will mightily roar against His fold.
He will give a shout, as those who tread the grapes,
against all the inhabitants of the earth.
31A noise shall come even to the remote parts of the earth,
for the Lord has a controversy with the nations; # Hos 4:1
He shall enter into judgment with all flesh. # Joel 3:2
He shall give those who are wicked to the sword,
says the Lord.
32Thus says the Lord of Hosts:
Behold, disaster shall go forth
from nation to nation, # Isa 34:2
and a great whirlwind shall be raised up # Jer 23:19
from the remote parts of the earth.
33The slain of the Lord on that day will be from one end of the earth even to the other end of the earth. # Isa 66:16 They will not be lamented or gathered or buried. # Ps 79:3 They will be as dung on the ground.
34Howl, you shepherds, and cry;
and wallow in the ashes, # Jer 6:26 you leaders of the flock.
For the days of your slaughter and of your dispersions are accomplished;
and you will fall like a choice vessel.
35The shepherds will have no way to flee,
nor the leaders of the flock to escape. # Job 11:20; Am 2:14
36A voice of the cry of the shepherds
and a howling of the leaders of the flock shall be heard,
for the Lord has devastated their pasture.
37The peaceable habitations are cut down
because of the fierce anger of the Lord. # Isa 27:10–11
38He has left His lair as the lion; # Jer 4:7; 5:6
for their land is desolate
because of the fierceness of the oppressor
and because of His fierce anger.
