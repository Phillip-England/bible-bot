Leviticus 10

10
The Profane Fire of Nadab and Abihu
1Then #Ex. 24:1, 9; Num. 3:2–4; 1 Chr. 24:2Nadab and Abihu, the sons of Aaron, #Lev. 16:12each took his censer and put fire in it, put incense on it, and offered #Ex. 30:9; 1 Sam. 2:17profane fire before the Lord, which He had not commanded them. 2So #Gen. 19:24; Num. 11:1; 16:35; Rev. 20:9fire went out from the Lord and devoured them, and they died before the Lord. 3And Moses said to Aaron, “This is what the Lord spoke, saying:
‘By those #Ex. 19:22; Lev. 21:6; Is. 52:11; Ezek. 20:41who come near Me
I must be regarded as holy;
And before all the people
I must be glorified.’ ”
So Aaron held his peace.
4Then Moses called Mishael and Elzaphan, the sons of Uzziel the uncle of Aaron, and said to them, “Come near, #Acts 5:6, 10carry your brethren from before the sanctuary out of the camp.” 5So they went near and carried them by their tunics out of the camp, as Moses had said.
6And Moses said to Aaron, and to Eleazar and Ithamar, his sons, “Do not uncover your heads nor tear your clothes, lest you die, and #Num. 1:53; 16:22, 46; 18:5; Josh. 7:1; 22:18, 20; 2 Sam. 24:1wrath come upon all the people. But let your brethren, the whole house of Israel, bewail the burning which the Lord has kindled. 7#Lev. 8:33; 21:12You shall not go out from the door of the tabernacle of meeting, lest you die, #Lev. 8:30for the anointing oil of the Lord is upon you.” And they did according to the word of Moses.
Conduct Prescribed for Priests
8Then the Lord spoke to Aaron, saying: 9#Gen. 9:21; (Prov. 20:1; 31:5); Is. 28:7; Ezek. 44:21; Hos. 4:11; Luke 1:15; (Eph. 5:18); 1 Tim. 3:3; Titus 1:7“Do not drink wine or intoxicating drink, you, nor your sons with you, when you go into the tabernacle of meeting, lest you die. It shall be a statute forever throughout your generations, 10that you may #Lev. 11:47; 20:25; Ezek. 22:26; 44:23distinguish between holy and unholy, and between unclean and clean, 11#Deut. 24:8; Neh. 8:2, 8; Jer. 18:18; Mal. 2:7and that you may teach the children of Israel all the statutes which the Lord has spoken to them by the hand of Moses.”
12And Moses spoke to Aaron, and to Eleazar and Ithamar, his sons who were left: #Num. 18:9“Take the grain offering that remains of the offerings made by fire to the Lord, and eat it without leaven beside the altar; #Lev. 21:22for it is most holy. 13You shall eat it in a #Num. 18:10holy place, because it is your due and your sons’ due, of the sacrifices made by fire to the Lord; for #Lev. 2:3; 6:16so I have been commanded. 14#Ex. 29:24, 26, 27; Lev. 7:30–34; Num. 18:11The breast of the wave offering and the thigh of the heave offering you shall eat in a clean place, you, your sons, and your #Lev. 22:13daughters with you; for they are your due and your sons’ #Num. 18:10due, which are given from the sacrifices of peace offerings of the children of Israel. 15#Lev. 7:29, 30, 34The thigh of the heave offering and the breast of the wave offering they shall bring with the offerings of fat made by fire, to offer as a wave offering before the Lord. And it shall be yours and your sons’ with you, by a statute forever, as the Lord has commanded.”
16Then Moses made careful inquiry about #Lev. 9:3, 15the goat of the sin offering, and there it was—burned up. And he was angry with Eleazar and Ithamar, the sons of Aaron who were left, saying, 17#Lev. 6:24–30“Why have you not eaten the sin offering in a holy place, since it is most holy, and God has given it to you to bear #Ex. 28:38; Lev. 22:16; Num. 18:1the guilt of the congregation, to make atonement for them before the Lord? 18See! #Lev. 6:30Its blood was not brought inside the holy place; indeed you should have eaten it in a holy place, #Lev. 6:26, 30as I commanded.”
19And Aaron said to Moses, “Look, #Lev. 9:8, 12this day they have offered their sin offering and their burnt offering before the Lord, and such things have befallen me! If I had eaten the sin offering today, #(Is. 1:11–15); Jer. 6:20; 14:12; Hos. 9:4; (Mal. 1:10, 13; 3:1–4)would it have been accepted in the sight of the Lord?” 20So when Moses heard that, he was content.
