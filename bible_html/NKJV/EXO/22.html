Responsibility for Property
1“If a man steals an ox or a sheep, and slaughters it or sells it, he shall #2 Sam. 12:6; Prov. 6:31; Luke 19:8restore five oxen for an ox and four sheep for a sheep. 2If the thief is found #Job 24:16; Matt. 6:19; 24:43; 1 Pet. 4:15breaking in, and he is struck so that he dies, there shall be #Num. 35:27no guilt for his bloodshed. 3If the sun has risen on him, there shall be guilt for his bloodshed. He should make full restitution; if he has nothing, then he shall be #Ex. 21:2; Matt. 18:25sold for his theft. 4If the theft is certainly#Ex. 21:16 found alive in his hand, whether it is an ox or donkey or sheep, he shall #Prov. 6:31restore double.
5“If a man causes a field or vineyard to be grazed, and lets loose his animal, and it feeds in another man’s field, he shall make restitution from the best of his own field and the best of his own vineyard.
6“If fire breaks out and catches in thorns, so that stacked grain, standing grain, or the field is consumed, he who kindled the fire shall surely make restitution.
7“If a man #Lev. 6:1–7delivers to his neighbor money or articles to keep, and it is stolen out of the man’s house, #Ex. 22:4if the thief is found, he shall pay double. 8If the thief is not found, then the master of the house shall be brought to the #Ex. 21:6, 22; 22:28; Deut. 17:8, 9; 19:17judges to see whether he has put his hand into his neighbor’s goods.
9“For any kind of trespass, whether it concerns an ox, a donkey, a sheep, or clothing, or for any kind of lost thing which another claims to be his, the #Deut. 25:1; 2 Chr. 19:10cause of both parties shall come before the judges; and whomever the judges condemn shall pay double to his neighbor. 10If a man delivers to his neighbor a donkey, an ox, a sheep, or any animal to keep, and it dies, is hurt, or driven away, no one seeing it, 11then an #Heb. 6:16oath of the Lord shall be between them both, that he has not put his hand into his neighbor’s goods; and the owner of it shall accept that, and he shall not make it good. 12But #Gen. 31:39if, in fact, it is stolen from him, he shall make restitution to the owner of it. 13If it is #Gen. 31:39torn to pieces by a beast, then he shall bring it as evidence, and he shall not make good what was torn.
14“And if a man borrows anything from his neighbor, and it becomes injured or dies, the owner of it not being with it, he shall surely make it good. 15If its owner was with it, he shall not make it good; if it was hired, it came for its hire.
Moral and Ceremonial Principles
16#Deut. 22:28, 29“If a man entices a virgin who is not betrothed, and lies with her, he shall surely pay the bride-price for her to be his wife. 17If her father utterly refuses to give her to him, he shall pay money according to the #Gen. 34:12; 1 Sam. 18:25bride-price of virgins.
18#Lev. 19:31; 20:6, 27; Deut. 18:10, 11; 1 Sam. 28:3–10; Jer. 27:9, 10“You shall not permit a sorceress to live.
19#Lev. 18:23; 20:15, 16; Deut. 27:21“Whoever lies with an animal shall surely be put to death.
20#Ex. 32:8; 34:15; Lev. 17:7; Num. 25:2; Deut. 17:2, 3, 5; 1 Kin. 18:40; 2 Kin. 10:25“He who sacrifices to any god, except to the Lord only, he shall be utterly destroyed.
21#Ex. 23:9; Deut. 10:19; Zech. 7:10“You shall neither mistreat a stranger nor oppress him, for you were strangers in the land of Egypt.
22#Deut. 24:17, 18; Prov. 23:10, 11; Jer. 7:6, 7; (James 1:27)“You shall not afflict any widow or fatherless child. 23If you afflict them in any way, and they #(Luke 18:7)cry at all to Me, I will surely #Deut. 10:17, 18; Ps. 18:6hear their cry; 24and My #Ps. 69:24wrath will become hot, and I will kill you with the sword; #Ps. 109:9your wives shall be widows, and your children fatherless.
25#Lev. 25:35–37“If you lend money to any of My people who are poor among you, you shall not be like a moneylender to him; you shall not charge him #Deut. 23:19, 20; Neh. 5:1–13; Ps. 15:5; Ezek. 18:8interest. 26#Deut. 24:6, 10–13; Job 24:3; Prov. 20:16; Amos 2:8If you ever take your neighbor’s garment as a pledge, you shall return it to him before the sun goes down. 27For that is his only covering, it is his garment for his skin. What will he sleep in? And it will be that when he cries to Me, I will hear, for I am #Ex. 34:6, 7gracious.
28#Eccl. 10:20“You shall not revile God, nor curse a #Acts 23:5ruler of your people.
29“You shall not delay to offer #Ex. 23:16, 19; Deut. 26:2–11; Prov. 3:9the first of your ripe produce and your juices. #Ex. 13:2, 12, 15The firstborn of your sons you shall give to Me. 30#Deut. 15:19Likewise you shall do with your oxen and your sheep. It shall be with its mother #Lev. 22:27seven days; on the eighth day you shall give it to Me.
31“And you shall be #Ex. 19:6; Lev. 11:44; 19:2holy men to Me: #Lev. 7:24; 17:15; Ezek. 4:14you shall not eat meat torn by beasts in the field; you shall throw it to the dogs.
